<h2>Ingredients With Quantity </h2>
<ul><li>1 Medium Beetroot (Around 140G/5Oz), Washed, Trimmed And Coarsely Grated</li><li>150G/5 1/2Oz Bulgur Wheat</li><li>1 Tsp Cumin Seeds</li><li>1 Tsp Flaked Sea Salt, Plus Extra To Season</li><li> 1/2 Vegetable Stock Cube</li><li>2 Tbsp Extra Virgin Olive Oil</li><li>1 Tbsp Balsamic Vinegar Or Lemon Juice</li><li>Pinch Cayenne Pepper</li><li> 1/2 Tsp Caster Sugar</li><li>Freshly Ground Black Pepper</li><li>2 Tbsp Extra Virgin Olive Oil, Plus Extra For Brushing</li><li>140G/5Oz Paneer, Cut Into 6 Slices</li><li>1 Tbsp Finely Chopped Flatleaf Parsley</li><li> 1/2 Lemon, Zest Only</li><li>1 Shredded Little Gem Lettuce Or Mixed Leaves, Lightly Dressed With Extra Virgin Olive Oil And Lemon Juice</li><li>Lemon Wedges</li></ul>

<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>Wash the beetroot and grate it finely. </li><li>Place the bulgur wheat in a sieve to drain off excess moisture. </li><li>Put the minced beetroot into the bowl with the bulgur wheat. </li><li>Grind the cumin seeds and seasonings, with a pinch of salt in a muslin bag. </li><li>Add the knead tsp of the coarse spice mixture and stock cube, and mix till well combined. </li><li>Place the balsamic vinegar in a separate container, and add the oil, cayenne, salt and pepper. </li><li>Mix well. </li><li>Pour over the bulgur wheat. </li><li>Mix all the ingredients together till well combined. </li><li>Leave to stand for a while, to develop flavours. </li><li>Now heat the virgin oil in a non stick pan over a medium heat and add in the paneer slices. </li><li>Cook the paneer slices for 2 3 min till golden brown on both sides. </li><li>Place the paneer in a bowl, and pour the dressing on top of the paneer. </li><li>Add the parsley, chives, lemon slices and toss well. </li><li>Set aside, and toss the lettuce leaves in the same way. </li><li>To serve divide the salad into serving plates, add in a little little gem lettuce leaf</li></ul>
