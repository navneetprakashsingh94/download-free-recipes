<h2>Ingredients With Quantity </h2>
<ul><li>200G/7Oz '00' Pasta Flour, Plus Extra For Dusting</li><li>2 Free-Range Eggs, Lightly Beaten</li><li>12 Large Fresh Basil Leaves, Plus 18-20 Small Basil Leaves, Plus Extra To Serve</li><li>225G/8Oz Buffalo-Milk Ricotta (Or Cows' Milk Ricotta)</li><li>60G/2 1/4Oz Toasted Pine Nuts, To Serve</li><li>Grated Parmesan (Or A Similar Vegetarian Hard Cheese), To Serve</li><li>50G/1 3/4Oz Fresh Basil Leaves, Chopped</li><li>10G/ 1/4Oz Toasted Pine Nuts</li><li>25G/1Oz Parmesan, Grated</li><li>1 Garlic Clove, Crushed</li><li>25Ml/1Fl Oz Extra Virgin Olive Oil</li><li>50Ml/2Fl Oz Olive Oil</li><li>Salt And Freshly Ground Black Pepper</li></ul>

<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>Start with the pesto. </li><li>Start by grinding the basil leaves, pine nuts, parmesan cheese, clove, salt and pepper, over a very low heat for 15 minutes. </li><li>Transfer to a food processor and blend in the oils. </li><li>Remove the pesto from the food processor and stir into the pesto. </li><li>Let this cool, and refrigerate. </li><li>Bring a large pan of water to the boil, then add salt for gnocchi. </li><li>Add the gnocchi to the boiling water, using a slotted spoon. </li><li>Cook until tender, about 10 to 12 minutes, then drain in a colander. </li><li>Toss together the pesto, ricotta cows milk ricotta, pine nuts, parmesan, and 3 eggs. </li><li>Taste and adjust the seasonings. </li><li>Add a touch of olive oil if needed. </li><li>Stir in the pesto, and serve. </li></ul>
