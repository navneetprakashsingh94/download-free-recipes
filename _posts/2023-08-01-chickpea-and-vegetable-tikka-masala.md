<h2>Ingredients With Quantity </h2>
<ul><li>1 Tsp Olive Oil</li><li>2 Small Onions (200G/7Oz In Total), Finely Chopped</li><li>2 X 400G Tins Chickpeas, Rinsed And Drained</li><li>4 Garlic Cloves, Grated</li><li>3 Tsp Ground Cumin</li><li>2 Tsp Ground Coriander</li><li>2 Tsp Sea Salt Flakes</li><li>30G/1Oz Coriander Leaves, Roughly Chopped</li><li>30G/1Oz Flatleaf Parsley Leaves, Roughly Chopped</li><li>40G/1 1/2Oz Plain Flour</li><li>Freshly Ground Black Pepper</li><li>Olive Oil Spray</li><li>200Ml/7Oz 0% Fat Plain Yoghurt</li><li>140G/5Oz Piece Cucumber, Grated</li><li>1 Small Garlic Clove, Finely Grated</li><li>2 Tbsp Finely Chopped Mint Leaves</li><li>Pinch Sea Salt</li><li>Pinch Granulated Sweetener (Optional)</li></ul>

<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>For the sauce</li><li>Mix all the ingredients together and set aside. </li><li>Preheat a small pan to medium heat. </li><li>In a mixing bowl, add the oil and saute the onions on medium heat for 5 minutes until golden. </li><li>Add the spices, salt, coriander, and parsley and toss to mix well. </li><li>Add the onions to the chickpeas. </li><li>Pour the chickpea mixture into a food processor and mix well. </li><li>Add the yogurt sauce and stir to combine. </li><li>Transfer mixture to a non stick frypan and saute on medium high heat for 2 3 minutes. </li><li>Pour the mixture into the bowl with the onions and chickpeas. </li><li>Season with the pepper. </li><li>Heat the oil in a frying pan. </li><li>In batches, pour the batter into the pan, and cook for 4 5 minutes on each side. </li><li>The tikka masala is ready when the edges are golden and the tikka is cooked through. </li></ul>
