<h2>Ingredients With Quantity </h2>
<ul><li>1.4Kg/3Lb 1Oz Collar Of Bacon, Skin On, Soaked In Cold Water Overnight In The Fridge Or In A Cool Place</li><li>1 Fresh Bay Leaf</li><li>10 Black Peppercorns</li><li>2 Cloves</li><li>1 Stick Cinnamon</li><li>1 Large Onion, Peeled</li><li>600Ml/1 Pint 2Fl Oz Good Cider</li><li>25G/1Oz Unsalted Butter</li><li>4 Large Garlic Cloves, Peeled</li><li>8 Small Shallots, Peeled But With Root End Intact</li><li>10 White Peppercorns</li><li>4 Medium Carrots, Sliced On The Diagonal To 5Mm/ 1/4In Thick</li><li>3 Sticks Celery, Sliced On The Diagonal To 5Mm/ 1/4In Thick</li><li>90G/3 1/2Oz Self-Raising Flour</li><li>Pinch Salt</li><li>1 Tsp English Mustard Powder</li><li>45G/1 1/2Oz Shredded Suet</li><li>1 Tbsp Chopped Fresh Parsley</li><li>Freshly Ground Black Pepper, To Taste</li><li>English Mustard, To Taste</li></ul>

<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>Put bacon in an enameled, heatproof dish. Add the bay leaf, peppercorns, cloves, cinnamon stick, and onion. Pour over the cider, cover and leave overnight. </li><li>Preheat oven to 425. </li><li>Heat butter in a saucepan and add the cloves, shallots, peppercorns, carrots, and celery. Cook until the vegetables are just tender. Remove from heat. </li><li>Meanwhile, mix the flour, salt, and mustard together. Rub in the suet until crumbly. Add the herbs and a generous sprinkling of pepper. Add the cider to the flour mixture, a little at a time, and mix well with your hands to make a soft dough. </li><li>Roll out the dough on a floured work surface to 1/4 inch thickness. Cut into 1/2 inch rounds using a 1/2 inch biscuit cutter. </li><li>Transfer to a baking sheet and bake for 20 minutes until golden. </li><li>Serve with a bowl of dijon mustard. </li></ul>
