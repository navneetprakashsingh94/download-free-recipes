<h2>Ingredients With Quantity </h2>
<ul><li>250Ml/9Fl Oz Vegetable Oil</li><li>10G/ 1/3 Oz Salt</li><li>5G/ 1/4Oz Cracked White Peppercorns</li><li>15G/ 1/2Oz Banana Shallots, Peeled And Finely Chopped</li><li>100Ml/3 1/2Fl Oz White Wine Vinegar</li><li>20G/ 3/4Oz Dijon Mustard</li><li>20G/ 3/4Oz English Mustard</li><li>3 Duck Eggs</li><li>20 Asparagus Spears, Peeled And Woody Ends Removed</li><li>50G/1 3/4Oz Unsalted Butter</li><li>2 Tbsp Roughly Chopped Fresh Flatleaf Parsley</li><li>Salt And Freshly Ground Black Pepper</li><li>2 Tbsp Olive Oil</li><li>20 Small Morel Mushrooms, Trimmed</li><li>100Ml/3 1/2Fl Oz Madeira</li></ul>

<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>Brine the duck eggs with a cup of water, salt and pepper. </li><li>Cool the eggs in cold water and peel. </li><li>In a blender, put the vinegar, mustards and a little water. </li><li>Blend until well blended. </li><li>Slowly add the remaining water, until the mixture has emulsified and the mustards are emulsified. </li><li>Add the eggs and puree. </li><li>Season the asparagus spears with salt and pepper. </li><li>Melt the butter in a pan and fry the asparagus spears. </li><li>Season to taste. </li><li>Heat the oil and fry the morels over medium heat for about 3 minutes. </li><li>Season with salt and pepper. </li><li>Add the madeira and bring to a boil. </li><li>Cook for about a minute, until mushrooms are soft. </li><li>Arrange asparagus on a warm platter. </li><li>Top with the eggs and mushroom sauce. </li><li>Garnish with parsley. </li></ul>
