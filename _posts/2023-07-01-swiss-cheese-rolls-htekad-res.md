<h2>Ingredients With Quantity </h2>
<ul><li>500G/1Lb 2Oz Strong Plain Flour, Plus Extra For Dusting</li><li>2 Tsp Salt</li><li>1 X 7G Sachet Of Dried Yeast</li><li>320Ml/11Fl Oz Lukewarm Water</li><li>Dried Breadcrumbs Or Semolina, For Dusting</li><li>80G/3Oz Gruyere Cheese, Grated</li><li>250G/9Oz Creme Fraiche</li><li>Salt And Freshly Ground Black Pepper</li><li>1 Large Red Onion, Thinly Sliced</li><li>140G/5Oz Salami, Cut Into Strips</li><li>Marjoram Leaves, To Finish</li></ul>

<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>Mix breadcrumbs, creme fraiche, salt and pepper and leave aside. </li><li>Put flour and a dash of salt in a bowl. Make a well in the center. Put the yeast into the well and slowly pour the water. Mix to make a dough, add a tbsp of water if needed. </li><li>Knead lightly to form a dough, place in a plastic bag and leave to rest in a warm place until doubled in size. </li><li>Divide into eight equal pieces and roll into balls. </li><li>Place on greased baking sheet and flatten slightly. Cover loosely with a cloth, then cover and leave to rise for another 20 minutes. </li><li>Meanwhile heat the fat in a frying pan, add the onions and saute until soft and translucent. </li><li>Remove from the heat and add to the risen rolls. </li><li>Place a slice of salami on each roll. Top with breadcrumbs, marjoram and grated cheese. </li><li>Place the rolls on a baking sheet, bake at 200 c until browned and cheese is hot through. </li></ul>
