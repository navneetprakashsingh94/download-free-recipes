<h2>Ingredients With Quantity </h2>
<ul><li>Butter, For Greasing</li><li>400Ml/14Fl Oz Full-Fat Milk</li><li>50G/1 3/4Oz Fresh White Breadcrumbs</li><li>4 Large Free-Range Eggs, Beaten</li><li>1 Tbsp Olive Oil</li><li>1Kg/2Lb 4Oz Lamb Mince</li><li>2 Onions, Finely Chopped</li><li>1 Bay Leaf</li><li>2 Garlic Cloves, Crushed</li><li>2 Tbsp Madras Curry Paste</li><li>250Ml/9Fl Oz Lamb Stock</li><li>2 Tbsp Worcestershire Sauce</li><li>2 Tbsp Mango Chutney</li><li>50G/1 3/4Oz Raisins</li><li>50G/1 3/4Oz Dried Apricots, Chopped</li><li>1 Tbsp Cider Vinegar</li></ul>

<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>Heat the butter in a large pan, add the milk and breadcrumbs and cook, stirring over medium heat until the breadcrumbs are soft and golden. </li><li>Transfer to a large bowl, let cool slightly, then stir in the eggs. Season. </li><li>Grease a loaf tin and line with greaseproof paper. Press the mixture into the tin, smooth the top and bake at 175 degrees c for 40 45 minutes, until cooked. </li><li>Heat the oil in a saucepan and cook the lamb mince for 3 4 minutes until the mince is browned, then add the onion and bay leaf and cloves and fry for a further 5 minutes. Stir in the madras paste, stock, cheddar sauce, chutney, raisins, apricots and vinegar. </li><li>Remove from the heat and allow to cool slightly. Stir in 1 tablespoon of the sauce and season with salt and pepper. Spoon the lamb mixture into the loaf tin. Bake for a further 15 minutes, until cooked through. </li></ul>
