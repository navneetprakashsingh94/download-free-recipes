<h2>Ingredients With Quantity </h2>
<ul><li>4 X 200G/7Oz Thick-Cut Rose Veal Loin Chops</li><li>4 Tbsp Extra Virgin Olive Oil</li><li>2 Tbsp Fennel Seeds</li><li>Large Bunch Fresh Sage, Roughly Torn</li><li>6-8 Sprigs Fresh Thyme, Broken Into Smaller Lengths</li><li>4 Large Sprigs Fresh Rosemary, Broken Into Smaller Lengths</li><li>2 Garlic Bulbs, Cut In Half Horizontally, Then Broken Into Smaller Pieces</li><li>2 Unwaxed Lemons, Rind Peeled From The Fruit In Long Strips</li><li>Freshly Ground Black Pepper</li><li>Sea Salt Flakes</li><li>Large Handful Finely Chopped Fresh Marjoram Leaves</li><li>5 Tbsp Extra Virgin Olive Oil</li><li> 1/2 Lemon, Juice Only, Plus Lemon Wedges, To Serve</li><li> 1/2 Tsp Sea Salt Flakes</li></ul>

<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>Mix the oil with the fennel seeds in a large bowl. Add the sage, thyme, savory, fennel, and fennel, and toss well. </li><li>Add the chops, and toss again. </li><li>Sprinkle with the salt, pepper, and marjoram. </li><li>Cover tightly, and marinate in the refrigerator for 2 to 3 hours. </li><li>Remove the chops, and remove the herb sprigs. </li><li>Grill or broil the chops or grill them or pan fry them to your liking. I prefer grill marks, which i personally prefer. </li><li>Serve the lamb chops with the herbs and garnish with lemon wedges and sea salt flakes. </li><li>Serve with a bed of the herbs in the center of the bowl. </li><li>Serve with a dill pickle on the side if you wish. </li></ul>
