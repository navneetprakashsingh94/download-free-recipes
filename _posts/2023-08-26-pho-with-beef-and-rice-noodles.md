<h2>Ingredients With Quantity </h2>
<ul><li>2 Star Anise</li><li>4 Cloves</li><li>2 Tbsp Coriander Seeds</li><li>1 Large Onion, Unpeeled, Halved</li><li>1 Large Thumb-Sized Piece Fresh Root Ginger, Unpeeled And Halved</li><li>1.5 Litres/2 3/4 Pints Beef Stock</li><li>1 Bone Marrow, Cleaned</li><li>3 Tbsp Fish Sauce</li><li>250G/9Oz Flat Rice Noodles</li><li>400G/14Oz Best-Quality Sirloin Steak, Fat Removed, Very Thinly Sliced</li><li>Large Handful Beansprouts</li><li>3 Spring Onions, Thinly Sliced</li><li>1 Red Chilli, Thinly Sliced</li><li>Small Handful Mint Leaves</li><li>Small Handful Basil Leaves</li><li>Small Handful Coriander Leaves</li><li>2 Limes, Quartered</li></ul>

<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>Combine the star anise, cloves, coriander seeds, onion and ginger in a mortar and pestle and crush to a paste. Heat the stock in a large saucepan and cook the paste for 3 4 minutes. </li><li>Add the marrow bone to the simmering stock and cook for 5 minutes. Add the fish sauce and 3 1/2 cups of the simmering stock and simmer for 1 hour. </li><li>Meanwhile, cook the rice noodles in boiling water for about 1 minute. Drain and rinse under cold water. Set aside. </li><li>In a pan, cook the beef in a little of the simmering stock, a few pieces at a time, until cooked through. Drain the beef and set aside. Heat the remaining stock and add the noodles. </li><li>When the marrow bone has begun to rise to the surface, add the beansprouts and saute for 30 seconds. </li><li>Season with salt and pepper and divide amongst 2 bowls. Top with the spring onions, chilli, herbs and beef. Serve with a squeeze of lime. </li></ul>
