<h2>Ingredients With Quantity </h2>
<ul><li>4 X 200G/7Oz Beef Fillet Steaks</li><li>Salt And Freshly Ground Black Pepper</li><li>2 Tbsp Vegetable Oil</li><li>115G/4Oz Butter</li><li>1 Onion, Skin On, Halved</li><li>150Ml/5Fl Oz Madeira</li><li>150Ml/5Fl Oz Red Wine</li><li>250Ml/9Fl Oz Beef Or Veal Stock</li><li>250G/9Oz Baby Spinach Leaves</li><li>1 Pinch Freshly Grated Nutmeg</li><li>175G/6Oz Duck Liver Pate</li><li>4 Slices Brioche</li></ul>

<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>Sprinkle the steak with salt and pepper and pan fry over medium high heat until cooked to your liking. </li><li></li><li>While the meat is frying, make the sauce. Melt the butter in a pan and saute the onion skin and carrots until translucent. </li><li>Add the madeira, wine, stock and seasonings and simmer until reduced by half. </li><li></li><li>Add the spinach and remove from heat. </li><li></li><li>Cut the brioche into diagonal slices. Arrange them on a cookie sheet and toast in a preheated 350 degree oven. Toast the bread in the oven. </li><li></li><li>To serve place 1 strip of the breast between 3 slices of brioche. </li><li>Top with some of the liver pate and top with the remaining slices of beef. </li><li></li><li>Transfer the pantocasse to a heated serving dish and pour the pan sauce over top. Garnish with fresh nutmeg, parsley and parsley. </li></ul>
