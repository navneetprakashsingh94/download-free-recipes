<h2>Ingredients With Quantity </h2>
<ul><li>2 Tbsp Groundnut Oil</li><li>2 Green Cardamoms (Or Black If You Can Get Them)</li><li>5Cm/2In Length Cassia Bark Or Cinnamon Stick</li><li>1 Medium Onion, Peeled And Sliced</li><li>255G/9Oz Rib-Eye Steak, Trimmed And Cut Into Thin Strips</li><li>1 Green Chilli, Chopped</li><li>2 Garlic Cloves, Peeled And Sliced</li><li> 1/4 Tsp Turmeric</li><li> 1/4 Tsp Salt</li><li> 1/2 Tsp Ground Cumin</li><li> 1/2 Tsp Ground Coriander</li><li>2 Tsp Tomato Puree</li><li> 1/2 Tsp Peeled And Grated Root Ginger</li><li>Pinch Of Garam Masala</li><li>30G/1Oz Fresh Coriander Leaves, Washed And Chopped, To Garnish</li></ul>

<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>1. Warm the oil in a heavy based pan, add the cardamom and cinnamon sticks and saute for a minute or two. </li><li>2. Add the sliced onion, beef and chilli. Continue cooking for 10 minutes or until the meat loses its pink colour. </li><li>3. Add all the remaining ingredients except the garam masala, stir well and then simmer for 15 minutes, or until the beef is tender. </li><li>4. Transfer the mixture to a blender, working in small batches if necessary, and blend until smooth. </li><li>5. Reheat gently and pour into a serving bowl and serve garnished with the coriander leaves. </li></ul>
