<h2>Ingredients With Quantity </h2>
<ul><li>6 Tbsp Olive Oil, Plus Extra For The Stuffing</li><li>5 Garlic Cloves, 2 Finely Sliced And 3 Left Whole</li><li>1 Red Chilli, Seeds Removed And Finely Sliced</li><li>1 Red Pepper, Seeds Removed And Chopped Into 1Cm/ 1/2In Pieces</li><li>400G Tin Chopped Tomatoes</li><li>1 Tbsp Red Wine Vinegar</li><li>125G/4 1/2Oz Walnuts</li><li> 1/2 Bunch Fresh Coriander, Leaves And Stalks Separated, Leaves Roughly Chopped</li><li> 1/2 Tsp Dried Chilli Flakes</li><li> 1/2 Tsp Cinnamon</li><li> 1/2 Tsp Ground Cumin</li><li> 1/2 Tsp Ground Coriander</li><li>10 Baby Aubergines</li><li>Sea Salt And Freshly Ground Black Pepper</li><li>500G/1Lb 2Oz Small Green Olives, Either Fresh Or In Brine, Drained</li><li>50Ml/2Fl Oz Extra Virgin Olive Oil</li><li>2 Lemons</li><li>3 Garlic Cloves, Crushed</li><li>2 Tsp Coriander Seeds, Roughly Crushed</li><li>Sea Salt</li><li>Few Sprigs Fresh Coriander, Finely Chopped, To Serve</li><li>Few Pinches Pul Biber Or Red Chilli Flakes, To Serve</li></ul>

<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>Preheat the oven to 350f/175c/gas mark 4. </li><li>Make the stuffing in a mortar, combine the cloves, chilli and pepper seeds and crush with the pestle to make a fine powder. </li><li>Add the chopped tomatoes and vinegar to the ground mixture and season with salt. </li><li>Add the walnuts, coriander leaves, chilli flakes, cinnamon, cumin and coriander to the paste. </li><li>Add the aubergines to the stuffing and mix together using your hands. </li><li>Arrange the aubergine in a round baking tray, sprinkle with salt and pepper and cover loosely with foil. </li><li>Bake for 30 minutes. </li><li>While the aubergines are cooking, make the brine. </li><li>Place all the ingredients into a saucepan over low heat and simmer for 10 minutes. </li><li>Drain the olives and combine with the brine in a bowl. </li><li>Allow to cool completely before using. </li></ul>
