<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>Prepare the rice in a saucepan, add cold water until the water covers the rice, bring to the boil and simmer for 10 minutes. Add the rice and continue to simmer uncovered for an additional 8 10 minutes. Check the rice with a fork for doneness or as you prefer it as the rice should not be crumbly, but tender. Remove the rice from the heat and leave to cool for a few minutes. </li><li></li><li>Meanwhile heat the oil in a pan and add the onions. When soft, add the cumin seeds, bay leaf, cinnamon, cardamom pods and salt and cook for a further 3 minutes until the onions are soft and translucent. Transfer the onions to a small bowl. </li><li></li><li>Preheat the oven to 190c/200c fan/gas 6. </li><li></li><li>Melt the butter and 1 tbsp oil in another saucepan over a medium heat. Once the butter has melted add the cumin seeds and the turmeric and stir well for 1 minute. Add the chopped ginger and sliced onions. Cook for 2 3 minutes until the onions start to brown. Add the remaining onion and the chillies and cook for 1 minute. Add the chopped methi leaves and cauliflower and cook for 2 minutes. </li><li></li><li>Stir in the potatoes and the garam masala. Increase the heat to high and cook until the potatoes are cooked and the cauliflower is tender and slightly golden.
