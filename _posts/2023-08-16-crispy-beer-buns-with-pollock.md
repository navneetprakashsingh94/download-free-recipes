<h2>Ingredients With Quantity </h2>

<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>To make the dough, put the flour, yeast and sugar in a large bowl. </li><li>Add the salt, vinegar and beer. Mix to make a smooth dough. Cover with a damp cloth and set aside in a warm spot for about 20 minutes. </li><li>For the filling, heat the oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the fish. Cook for 2 3 minutes each side, or until just cooked through. Drain on paper towels and season with sea salt. </li><li>For the buns, melt the butter and add the bread. Cook until lightly browned. </li><li>For the mustard hollandaise, whisk the egg yolks, mustard and oil in a large bowl. Mix in the lemon juice. Add the salt and pepper to taste. Add the wheat germ and mustard. Using a wooden spoon, gradually add the yeast and beer. Mix until well combined. Let the dough rise in a warm place until doubled in volume. </li><li>To bake the buns, cut the bread into wedges. Spread a glug of butter onto the inside of a cake tin. Place the bread wedges in the tin, buttered side down. Carefully pour a scant 1/4 cup of the mustard hollandaise on the bread. Fill each wedge with some peas and top with the fish. Bake on the lower shelf of a preheated oven at 220 c/200 f for 5 8 minutes, or until golden and crispy. Serve hot with the remaining mustard hollandaise. </li></ul>
