<h2>Ingredients With Quantity </h2>
<ul><li>600G/1Lb 5Oz Diced Lamb Leg</li><li>2 Tsp Tatli Biber Salcasi (Turkish Sweet Pepper Paste)</li><li> 1/2 Tsp Pul Biber (Turkish Chilli Flakes, Also Known As Aleppo Pepper)</li><li>4 Lavas (Turkish Flatbread) Or Wraps</li><li>Salt And Black Pepper</li><li>2 Tbsp Vegetable Oil, Plus Extra For Frying</li><li>15G/ 1/2Oz Butter</li><li>400G/14Oz Vine Tomatoes, Chopped</li><li>2 Garlic Cloves, Crushed</li><li>1 Heaped Tsp Tatli Biber Salcasi (Turkish Sweet Pepper Paste)</li><li>Pinch Of Sugar (Optional)</li><li>2 Tbsp Chopped Fresh Mint</li><li>50G/1 3/4Oz Butter</li><li>2 Garlic Cloves, Crushed</li><li>Yoghurt Dip, Tzatziki Or Cacik</li><li>Mixed Salad Leaves</li><li>Pomegrante Seeds, To Garnish</li></ul>

<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>First, prepare the sauce. Mix the tatli biber paste with the pul biber salt and the pul biber chilli flakes. Brush half over the lamb and marinate for one hour. </li><li>Prepare the wraps by first tearing each wrap into 4 and placing 1 tablespoon of olive oil in each wrap. Take a piece of lamb and place into the wrap and wrap it tightly. Repeat until the wrap is completely covered. </li><li>Now, make the tomato mint sauce. Slice the tomatoes in half lengthwise, remove the seeds, sprinkle with a little salt pepper and fry in a little oil in a pan with the cloves until fragrant. Remove from the pan and leave them to cool. </li><li>Cut the mint leaves in half. </li><li>Heat up the butter in a saucepan, add the chopped mint leaves and the sweet paste and fry until soft. Add the chopped tomatoes and a pinch of sugar. Simmer for 5 minutes and season with salt pepper. Let cool slightly. </li><li>Heat up the yoghurt dip. Toss the tzatziki cacik with the warm tomato mint sauce. </li><li>To serve, place a mound of pomegrante salad leaves on each plate and place the lavas wraps on top. Add the tomato mint sauce and serve. </li></ul>
