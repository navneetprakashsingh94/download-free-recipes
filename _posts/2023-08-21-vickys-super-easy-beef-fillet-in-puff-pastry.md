<h2>Ingredients With Quantity </h2>
<ul><li>1 Fillet Of Beef, Roughly 1.35Kg/3Lb</li><li>Dash Olive Oil</li><li>15G/ 1/2Oz Unsalted Butter</li><li>4 Thin Shop-Bought Pancakes</li><li>1 Pack (Roughly 375G) Ready Rolled, All-Butter Puff Pastry</li><li>175G/6Oz Chicken Liver Pate</li><li>1 Small Free-Range Egg, Beaten</li><li>55G/2Oz Unsalted Butter</li><li>150G/5Oz Shallots, Finely Diced</li><li>250G/9Oz Flat Black Or Shiitake Mushrooms, Roughly Chopped</li><li>3 Tbsp Double Cream</li><li>Salt And Pepper</li></ul>

<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>Heat oil and butter in a large frying pan on medium high heat</li><li>Cook beef on both sides until medium rare, approximately 4 minutes each side, depending on size</li><li>Slice crossways into bite size pieces</li><li>Lay pancakes in the centre of each pancake. </li><li>Place a piece of beef on each pancake and top with chicken liver pate</li><li>Cover the puff pastry with the beaten egg</li><li>Cook on medium heat for 4 minutes each side for medium rare meat</li><li>Remove and serve hot on a hot plate topped with fresh mushroom sauce</li><li>For the sauce melt butter in a frying pan. </li><li>Saute shallots and mushrooms till softened</li><li>Add cream and season with salt and pepper</li><li>Remove from heat and let cool. </li><li>If sauce is too thick, add more cream until it is the consistency you like and it will still be a little runny. </li><li>If it is too runny it can be heated up</li><li>Serve hot with steaks</li></ul>
