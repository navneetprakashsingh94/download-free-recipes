<h2>Ingredients With Quantity </h2>
<ul><li>300G/10 1/2Oz Caster Sugar</li><li>300Ml/10 1/2Fl Oz Water</li><li>Half Lemon, Juice Only</li><li>4 Tsp Liquid Glucose</li><li>400G/14Oz Blackberries</li><li>75G/2 1/2Oz Caster Sugar</li><li>1 Tbsp Water</li><li>400G/14Oz Ready-Rolled Puff Pastry</li><li>250G/9Oz Apples, Peeled And Sliced Into Wedges</li><li>125G/4Oz Demerera Sugar</li><li>Fresh Mint Leaves, To Garnish</li></ul>

<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>Make syrup by heating the sugar, water, lemon juice and liquid glucose. If the mixture is too thin, add a little water. </li><li>Prepare blackberry puree by combining the blackberries, sugar and water in a pan and simmer over a low heat for about 10 minutes until the blackberries have burst. Strain and return to the pan and cook until reduced by half. </li><li>Lay the pastry sheets out on a flat work surface, using a rolling pin to roll out and cut out 12 sheets of pastry. </li><li>Layer the apple wedges on the pastry and place about 1 tablespoon of blackberry puree on top of each. Brush the edges of the pastry with water and fold the pastry over to make a half moon. Seal the edges by pinching them together with a fork. Cut 4 slits in the top of the pastry to allow steam to escape. </li><li>Heat the oven to 375 degrees f. </li><li>Sprinkle demerera sugar over the apples and bake for 10 minutes. Cool and cut into squares. </li><li>Garnish with fresh mint leaves and serve warm with ice cream. </li></ul>
