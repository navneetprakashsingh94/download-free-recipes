<h2>Ingredients With Quantity </h2>
<ul><li>2 Tbsp Olive Oil</li><li>25G/1Oz Unsalted Butter</li><li>750G/1Lb 10Oz Stewing Steak, Cut Into 2-3Cm/ 3/4-1 1/4In Pieces</li><li>2 Tbsp Plain Flour</li><li>2 Garlic Cloves, Chopped</li><li>250G/9Oz Round Shallots, Peeled And Left Whole</li><li>150Ml/5 1/2Fl Oz Red Wine</li><li>500Ml/18Fl Oz Beef Stock</li><li>2 Fresh Bay Leaves</li><li>3 Tbsp Fresh Thyme Leaves</li><li>Worcestershire Sauce, To Taste</li><li>Salt And Black Pepper</li><li>125G/4 1/2Oz Plain Flour</li><li>1 Tsp Baking Powder</li><li>Pinch Salt</li><li>60G/2 1/4Oz Suet</li><li>4 Medium Carrots, Peeled And Left Whole</li><li>50G/1 3/4Oz Unsalted Butter</li><li>1 Tbsp Sugar</li></ul>

<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>Heat the oil and butter in a large heavy based frying pan. Dredge beef in the flour, shaking off the excess. Add beef to the pan, cooking in two batches, adding more butter and oil as necessary. Cook meat until browned. Sprinkle with cloves and set aside. </li><li>Melt remaining butter in the pan. Add shallots and cook until golden. Add the wine and simmer for a few minutes. Return the beef to the pan with the stock and seasonings. Simmer, covered, for about 1 hour, or until the beef is tender. </li><li>Meanwhile, to make the dumplings, mix together the flour, baking powder and salt in a large bowl. Combine the suet, carrots, butter and sugar in a food processor. Process until the carrots are pureed and the mixture resembles fine breadcrumbs. Stir the carrot mixture into the flour mixture. </li><li>Drop by spoonfuls onto the simmering beef stew. Cover the pot and simmer for 10 minutes, or until the dumplings are cooked through. </li></ul>
