<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>Start by sprinkling each fruit with a tablespoon of the whiskey and leave to soak overnight. </li><li>Then the following day, place the tea, remaining whiskey and the soaked fruit into a bowl and mix with a wooden spoon. </li><li>Break the yeast into a bowl and pour over the milk. </li><li>Cover with cling film, then leave to proof for 30 minutes. </li><li>Combine the flora, sugar, spices and eggs in a bowl and mix with a wooden spoon until combined. </li><li>Add the yeast to the milk and egg and mix until it is fully incorporated. </li><li>Preheat the oven to 200c/400f/gas 6. </li><li>Pour half of the mixture over the fruit and mix with your hands. </li><li>Next, mix in the remaining fruit and the remaining mixture, followed by the honey and butter. </li><li>Then knead with your hands until smooth, elastic and supple but still fairly soft. </li><li>Do not overwork the dough as this will result in soft, tough scones. </li><li>Shape the dough into small balls and put them onto a baking tray lined with baking paper. </li><li>Once well risen, cut a x in the top of each and allow to rest for 10 minutes. </li><li>Brush with a little melted butter. </li><li>Bake for about 35 minutes until golden brown. </li><li>Leave to cool on the baking tray for 10 minutes and serve with a</li></ul>
