<h2>Ingredients With Quantity </h2>
<ul><li>5-6 Braeburn Apples, Peeled, Cored And Sliced</li><li>50G/1 3/4Oz Light Brown Sugar</li><li>1 Lemon, Juice Only</li><li>150G/5 1/2Oz Unsalted Butter</li><li>100G/3 1/2Oz Caster Sugar</li><li>2 Free-Range Eggs</li><li>35G/1 1/4Oz Plain Flour</li><li>1 Vanilla Pod, Seeds Removed</li><li>1 Ready-Made 22-Cm/8 1/2In Sweet Shortcrust Pastry Case</li><li>Handful Pecans</li><li>120G/4 1/2Oz Caster Sugar</li><li>1 Tsp Calvados</li><li>150Ml/5Fl Oz Double Cream</li><li>250G/9Oz Clotted Cream</li></ul>

<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>Preheat the oven to 180c 350f. </li><li>Place a sheet of baking paper in a 10 inch springform cake tin. </li><li>Line the pastry case with baking parchment and butter the base and sides. </li><li>For the pastry case, place the pastry case, parchment paper and apples in the pastry case. </li><li>Sprinkle with a mixture of the sugar and lemon juice. </li><li>Place in the oven and cook for 15 20 minutes or until golden brown. </li><li>For the filling, beat the butter and sugar together in a bowl with an electric mixer until pale and creamy. </li><li>Add the eggs, one at a time, beating well after each addition. </li><li>Add the flour and vanilla seeds and beat until just combined. </li><li>Set aside. </li><li>Remove the pastry case from the oven. </li><li>Remove the pastry case, parchment paper and apples from the oven. </li><li>Pour in the filling. </li><li>Return to the oven for a further 20 25 minutes or until just set in the middle. </li><li>Remove from the oven and allow to cool in the pan for 30 minutes. </li><li>Remove the tart from the pan and allow to cool completely. </li><li>For the caramelized apples, place the pecans, sugar and calvados or apple brandy in a small frying pan and gently stir over a medium heat until the sugar has dissolved and the liquid has reduced. </li><li>Carefully pour the caramel into a heatproof bowl and leave to cool. </li><li>For the cream, place the cream into a saucepan and bring just to the boil over a low heat, stirring constantly. </li><li>Keep it at a</li></ul>
