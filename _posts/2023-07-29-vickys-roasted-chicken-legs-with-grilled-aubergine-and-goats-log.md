<h2>Ingredients With Quantity </h2>
<ul><li>2 Chicken Legs</li><li>1 Onion, Chopped</li><li>2 Celery Sticks, Chopped</li><li>1 Bay Leaf</li><li>2 Thyme Sprigs</li><li>2 Garlic Cloves</li><li> 1/2 Lemon, Cut Into Quarters</li><li>250Ml/9Fl Oz White Wine</li><li>Salt And Freshly Ground Black Pepper</li><li>2 Tbsp Pine Nuts, Toasted</li><li>1-2 Tbsp Sumac</li><li>Handful Fresh Flatleaf Parsley, Chopped</li><li>1 Bunch Fresh Basil, Chopped</li><li>3 Tbsp Olive Oil</li><li>1 Garlic Clove, Minced</li><li>1 Lemon, Juice Only</li><li>1 Orange, Juice Only</li><li>1 Aubergine, Cut In Half Lengthways</li><li>3 Tbsp Runny Honey</li><li>3 Tbsp Olive Oil</li><li>2 Garlic Cloves, Crushed</li><li>1 Tbsp Chopped Fresh Coriander</li><li>1 Green Chilli, Finely Sliced</li><li>1 Tsp Cumin Seeds, Crushed</li><li>1 Tsp Coriander Seeds, Crushed</li><li>150G/5 1/2Oz Goats' Cheese Log, Sliced</li><li>1 Apple, Cored And Julienned</li><li>Squeeze Lemon Juice</li><li>Few Sprigs Fresh Coriander</li></ul>

<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>For the chicken, place the chicken legs, onion, celery, bay leaf, thyme, cloves, lemon zest, lemon juice and wine in a large baking dish. </li><li>Cover and bring to the boil, lower the heat and simmer for 25 30 minutes or until the chicken is cooked through. </li><li>Remove from the oven and leave to rest covered in a warm place for 10 minutes, then pull the legs to serve with the aubergine. </li><li>For the dressing, place the pine nuts in a small bowl and crush them slightly with a hammer. </li><li>Add the sumac, parsley, basil, oil, clove, lemon and orange juices, salt and pepper to taste, and mix well. </li><li>For the aubergine, cut the aubergine lengthways into thick slices about 1/4 inch thick. </li><li>Heat a hot griddle pan and cook the aubergine over a high heat for 15 minutes turning as necessary. </li><li>Remove the aubergine from the pan, drain and add the honey, oil, cloves, coriander and chilli and season to taste. </li><li>Cook over a low heat until thickened, then season to taste. </li><li>Remove from heat. </li><li>Heat a small frying pan and toast the cumin and coriander</li></ul>
