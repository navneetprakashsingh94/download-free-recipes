<h2>Ingredients With Quantity </h2>
<ul><li>4 Bramley Apples (Or 125Ml/4Fl Oz Apple Juice If You Do Not Have A Juicer)</li><li>Sugar, To Taste</li><li>2 Bramley Apples, Peeled, Cored And Cut In Half</li><li>100G/3 1/2Oz Butter</li><li>50G/1 3/4Oz Demerara Sugar</li><li>110G/3 3/4Oz Raisins</li><li>50Ml/1 3/4Fl Oz Double Cream</li><li>110G/3 1/2Oz Soft Brown Sugar</li><li>5G Ground Mixed Spice</li><li>1 Lemon, Zest Only</li><li>1 Orange, Zest Only</li><li>250Ml/9Fl Oz Milk</li><li>250Ml/9Fl Oz Cream</li><li>50G/1 3/4Oz Fresh Douglas Fir Pine Needles</li><li>200G/7Oz Pasteurised Egg Yolk, Or Fresh Egg Yolks</li><li>80G/2 3/4Oz Caster Sugar</li></ul>

<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>Make the bramley apple compote place the apples in a saucepan with the juice, sugar and a little water. </li><li>Simmer over medium heat, covered, until the apples are tender but not falling apart. </li><li>Remove from the heat and let cool. </li><li>Add the butter, demerara sugar, raisins and cream and blend together. </li><li>For the creme fraiche pour the milk and the cream into a saucepan and add the pine needles. </li><li>Bring to the boil over high heat then reduce the heat to low and simmer for 10 minutes. </li><li>Pour the sauce through a sieve into a bowl, then stir in the egg yolks, sugar and a pinch of salt. </li><li>Cover the base of a 24 centimeter cake tin with 2 tablespoons of the warm cider. </li><li>Cover the base of the tin with the brioche base. </li><li>Cover the brioche base with one third of the whipped cream and top with half of the bramley apple compote. </li><li>Repeat the process with the remaining whipped cream and bramley apple compote, ending with the final drizzle of cider. </li><li>Cover and refrigerate until thoroughly chilled. </li><li>Serve with the creme fraiche. </li></ul>
