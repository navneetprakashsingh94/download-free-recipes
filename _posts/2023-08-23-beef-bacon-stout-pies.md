<h2>Ingredients With Quantity </h2>
<ul><li>900G/2Lb Stewing Beef, Trimmed, Cut Into Rough 3Cm/1 1/4In Cubes</li><li>2-3 Tbsp Sunflower Oil</li><li>3 Long Shallots, Quartered</li><li>2 Garlic Cloves, Finely Chopped</li><li>125G/4 1/2Oz Smoked, Rindless, Streaky Bacon Rashers, Cut Into 1Cm/ 1/2In Strips</li><li>1 Tbsp Roughly Chopped Thyme Leaves</li><li>2 Bay Leaves</li><li>330Ml/11Fl Oz Stout</li><li>400Ml/14Fl Oz Beef Stock (Made With One Stock Cube)</li><li>Flaked Sea Salt And Freshly Ground Black Pepper</li><li>2 Tbsp Cornflour, Blended With 2 Tbsp Water To Make A Smooth Paste</li><li>8 Oysters, Freshly Shucked</li><li>400G/14Oz Plain Flour, Plus Extra For Rolling</li><li> 1/4 Tsp Salt</li><li>250G/9Oz Butter, Frozen For At Least 2 Hours</li><li>1 Free-Range Egg, Beaten, To Glaze</li></ul>

<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>Heat the oil in a large saucepan, add the beef cubes and cook, stirring for 3 mins, then transfer the meat to a plate and cook the shallots and cloves in the oil for 5 mins, until softened. Return the meat to the pan along with the bacon, thyme, bay leaves and beer. Bring to a boil, cover and simmer for 2 hours. </li><li>Remove the beef from the pan and strain the sauce through a fine sieve into a large saucepan. Bring to a boil and whisk in the stock cube and season with salt and pepper. Mix in the cornflour and bring to a boil. Cook for 5 mins, until thickened, then season with pepper. Remove from the heat. </li><li>Meanwhile, place the oysters in their liquor and let stand for 1 min. Drain well. </li><li>Sift the flour, salt and butter into a large bowl. Add the egg and mix with your hands until a rough dough forms. </li><li>Divide the dough into four equal portions, rolling each into a 12 inch circle. </li><li>Place 1 tbsp of beef filling in the centre of each dough circle, leaving a 2 inch border. Cut a small hole in the centre of each pie to allow the steam to escape. </li><li>Brush the edges of the pie with water. Place a teaspoon of oysters in the centre of each pie and cover each pie with a 2 inch border. Seal with a fork and pinch to seal. Brush the tops with more water. </li><li>Bring a large saucepan of salted water to a boil and add the pies. Reduce the heat</li></ul>
