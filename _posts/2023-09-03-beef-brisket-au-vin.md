<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>1 season the brisket with salt and pepper. </li><li>2 heat the oil in a large ovenproof pot over med high heat. </li><li>Brown the brisket on both sides in batches. </li><li>Transfer to the oven and cook, turning occasionally, until lightly browned, 15 20 mins. </li><li>Transfer to a plate. </li><li>3 add the bacon to the pot and cook, stirring, until lightly browned. </li><li>4 add the onion and cook, stirring, until softened. </li><li>5 return the brisket to the pot along with the tomato puree, stock, olives and herbs. </li><li>6 bring the liquid to a simmer over medium heat, reduce the heat to low, cover and cook until the brisket is tender, about 3 hours. </li><li>7 when it s cooked, remove the brisket from the pan and set aside. </li><li>8 strain the cooking liquid through a fine mesh strainer over a heat proof bowl or another container and discard the solids. </li><li>Reheat the brisket in the pan before you re ready to serve it. </li><li>9 add the wine, brandy, vinegar, herbs, zest, cloves, peppercorns and veal shanks to the saucepan and bring to the boil. </li><li>Transfer to a large heatproof bowl and cover tightly with plastic wrap. </li><li>10 remove the meat from the fridge. </li><li>15 when cool enough to handle, shred or shred the meat, discarding any excess fat and tendons. </li><li></li></ul>
