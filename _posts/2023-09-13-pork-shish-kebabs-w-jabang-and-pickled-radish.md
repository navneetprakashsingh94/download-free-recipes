<h2>Ingredients With Quantity </h2>
<ul><li>6 Blood Oranges, Juice Only</li><li>300Ml/10Fl Oz Balsamic Vinegar</li><li>2 Onions, Finely Chopped</li><li>100G/3 1/2Oz Fresh Galangal, Peeled And Finely Chopped</li><li>3 Red Chillies, Deseeded And Finely Chopped</li><li>4 Bay Leaves</li><li>10 Garlic Cloves, Chopped</li><li>2Kg/4Lb 8Oz Pork Belly</li><li>1 X 500Ml/18Fl Oz Bottle Sake</li><li>100G/3 1/2Oz Butter, Diced</li><li>125Ml/4Fl Oz Rice Vinegar</li><li>1 Red Onion, Peeled And Quartered</li><li>1 Bunch Pink Radishes, Stalks Removed</li><li>1 Small Mooli, Ends Removed</li><li>10G Mustard Seeds</li><li>25G/1Oz Sugar</li><li>5G Ground Black Cardamom</li><li>5G Dried Red Chilli Flakes</li></ul>

<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>Put all the ingredients for the brine in a large bowl and mix to get everything mixed. </li><li>Add the pork and cover with water and leave overnight. </li><li>The next day, take the pork out of the brine and place in a roasting tin. </li><li>Pour over the marinade of its cooking liquid and roast in the oven at 200c, gas mark 5 for approx 1 hour. </li><li>Turn the pork over and roast for a further 20 minutes or until cooked. </li><li>In the meantime, make a marinade of the wetter beer and water, and pour over the butter. </li><li>Marinade the meat while it is in the fridge for at least 4 hours. </li><li>If you have leftover wine, you can boil it and save it. </li><li>If the sauce is too thin, heat the rice vinegar in a pan. </li><li>Then add a pinch of salt. </li><li>Slice the onions. </li><li>Slice the radishes. </li><li>Put the pork in a pan with the mustard seeds, caster sugar, cardamom and chilli flakes and heat until melted. </li><li>Put on the skewers and pour the oranges, cardamom and radishes on the skewers, and the pickled onions on the skewers. </li><li>Serve hot. </li><li>Bon appetite</li></ul>
