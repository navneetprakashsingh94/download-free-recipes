<h2>Ingredients With Quantity </h2>
<ul><li>2 Tbsp White Wine Vinegar</li><li>4 Duck Eggs</li><li>16 Asparagus Spears, Stems Peeled And Trimmed</li><li>8 Slices Air-Cured Ham, Ideally British (Alternatively, Use Serrano Or Parma Ham)</li><li>125G/4 1/2Oz Unsalted Butter</li><li>50Ml/2Fl Oz White Wine Vinegar</li><li>1 Shallot, Finely Chopped</li><li> 1/2 Tsp Whole Black Peppercorns</li><li>2 Large Egg Yolks</li><li>Salt And Freshly Ground Black Pepper</li></ul>

<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>In a medium saucepan, bring 1 tablespoon of the vinegar to a boil. Poach the eggs for 8 minutes. Remove the eggs from the vinegar and set aside. </li><li>While the eggs are poaching, cook the asparagus in a pan of boiling water for 1 minute. Drain and transfer to a bowl. Season with salt and pepper. Cover the bowl and allow the asparagus to cool. </li><li>Bring the remaining 1/4 cup vinegar and 1/4 cup water to a boil in a medium saucepan. Add the asparagus to the water and cook for 1 minute. Remove from the heat. </li><li>For the sauce, melt 2 sticks of butter in a saucepan. Add the vinegar, shallot and peppercorns. Cook over medium heat until the vinegar has reduced by a third. </li><li>Meanwhile, in a metal bowl, beat the egg yolks with 1 tablespoon of butter until they become pale in color. Slowly pour 1 cup of the vinegar mixture into the egg mixture, whisking constantly. Gradually add the remaining butter, whisking constantly, until the sauce is thick enough to coat the back of a spoon. Taste and add salt and pepper if desired. </li><li>To assemble, fill a large, shallow bowl with cold water. Dip the asparagus in the vinegar water to coat and transfer to the bowl of ice water. Using your hands, gently slip the eggs into the water. Cook for 1 minute, turn and cook for 1 minute longer. </li><li>For the assembly, lay a ham slice into the center of a plate and garnish with a poached egg. Spoon the sauce over the top, placing some asparagus over the eggs. </li></ul>
