<h2>Ingredients With Quantity </h2>
<ul><li>140G/5Oz Soft Margarine (Or Butter, At Room Temperature), Plus Extra For Greasing</li><li>125G/4 1/2Oz Speculoos Biscuits (Approximately 16 Biscuits)</li><li>240G/8 3/4Oz Smooth Speculoos Spread, Such As Biscoff</li><li>120G/4 1/2Oz Soft Light Brown Sugar</li><li>140G/5Oz Self-Raising Flour</li><li>1 Tsp Baking Powder</li><li>4 Free-Range Eggs</li><li>3 Tbsp Greek-Style Yoghurt</li><li>150G/5 1/2Oz Butter, At Room Temperature</li><li>250G/9Oz Icing Sugar, Sieved</li><li>100G/3 1/2Oz Smooth Speculoos Spread, Such As Biscoff</li></ul>

<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>Grease round tin or a loaf tin. </li><li>Put the speculoos biscuits, speculoos spread, sugar, flour and baking powder into a bowl. </li><li>Mix well together. </li><li>Beat the eggs and yoghurt together until well combined. </li><li>Add the egg mixture to the dry mixture and mix well. </li><li>Pour the batter into the prepared tin and bake for 40 minutes in an oven preheated to 180c/350f or until golden brown. </li><li>Let the cake cool completely before frosting. </li><li>To make the frosting cream the speculoos spread and butter until smooth. </li><li>Add the icing sugar and mix well. </li><li>Spread on cooled cake. </li><li>The cake is much more delicious with cream cheese icing. </li><li>This recipe can be changed to make speculoos biscuit cake. </li><li>You can cut the biscuits into slices and add for the end. </li><li>It s the best way to use up your favourite biscuits. </li><li>Just mix the ingredients together and have the cake ready</li></ul>
