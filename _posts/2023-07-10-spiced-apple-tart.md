<h2>Ingredients With Quantity </h2>
<ul><li>300G/10 1/2Oz Plain Flour</li><li>200G/7Oz Brown Sugar</li><li>200G/7Oz Unsalted Butter, Cubed And Softened To Room Temperature, Plus Extra For Greasing</li><li>Pinch Salt</li><li>75G/3Oz Unsalted Butter, Plus Extra For Buttering</li><li>1 Kg/2Lb 4Oz Eating Apples (Such As Russet Or Cox), Peeled, Cored And Chopped Into Large Chunks</li><li>150G/5Oz Caster Sugar</li><li>5 Star Anise</li><li>1 Cinnamon Stick</li><li>Creme Fraiche</li><li>Pomegranate Seeds</li></ul>

<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>In a bowl, mix the flour, sugar, butter and salt together, working the butter into the flour mixture with your hands or a pastry blender until it forms a dough. If the dough seems too dry add the remaining butter, a tablespoon at a time. </li><li>Place the dough on a floured surface, knead lightly and shape into a disc. Wrap the dough in plastic and refrigerate for 30 minutes. </li><li>Meanwhile, melt the butter in a pan. Cook the apples over medium heat for about 7 minutes, stirring occasionally. </li><li>Add the sugar, star anise, cinnamon stick and a pinch of salt. Cook for another 5 minutes. Remove from heat and let cool. </li><li>For the pastry, roll the chilled dough to the size of a 6. 5cm diameter tart or tart pan with a removable base. Press into the bottom and sides. </li><li>Transfer the pastry to the tart pan with the sides. Bake in a preheated oven at 180 c for 5 minutes. </li><li>Turn off the oven and remove the pan from the oven. Spread a generous amount of the apples and a spoonful of the creme fraiche on top. Sprinkle with pomegranate seeds and bake for a further 15 minutes, or until the apples and creme fraiche are a deep golden brown. Serve warm. </li></ul>
