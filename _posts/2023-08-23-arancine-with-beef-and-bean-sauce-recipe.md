<h2>Ingredients With Quantity </h2>
<ul><li>350G/12Oz Dried Black-Eyed Beans</li><li>900G/2Lb Sirloin Steak, Cut Into 1Cm/ 1/2 In Cubes</li><li>325Ml/11 1/2Fl Oz Vegetable Oil</li><li>1 Large Onion, Chopped</li><li>4 Garlic Cloves, Finely Chopped</li><li>4Cm/2In Piece Fresh Root Ginger, Grated</li><li>1 Tsp Dried Chilli Flakes</li><li>2 X 400G Tins Chopped Tomatoes</li><li>2 Tbsp Tomato Puree</li><li>2 Beef Stock Cubes</li><li>2 Sprigs Fresh Thyme</li><li>1 Scotch Bonnet Chilli, Chopped</li><li>3 Ripe Plantain, Peeled And Sliced</li><li>Salt And Freshly Ground Black Pepper</li></ul>

<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>Sort and wash the beans. </li><li>Place them in a pan and cover with water. </li><li>Leave to soak overnight. </li><li>Rinse the beans again and drain. </li><li>Remove any pips or possibly stones. </li><li>Heat the oil in a pan and brown the beef in it. </li><li>Remove beef and set aside. </li><li>Add in the onion, cloves, ginger, chilli, tomato, puree, tomatoes, stock cube, ground peppercorns, peppercorns and thyme. </li><li>Stir together to break up the tomato flakes. </li><li>Add in the steak and beans. </li><li>Stir together and bring to the boil. </li><li>Cover and reduce the heat. </li><li>Simmer for 45 min. </li><li>Stir occasionally. </li><li>Add in the sliced potato and plantain and cook for another 40 min. </li><li>Taste and season with salt and pepper. </li><li>Serve with rice. </li></ul>
