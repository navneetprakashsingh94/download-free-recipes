<h2>Ingredients With Quantity </h2>
<ul><li>150G/5Oz Urid Dal</li><li>50G/2Oz Mung Beans</li><li>480Ml/17Fl Oz Water</li><li>3 Tbsp Olive Oil</li><li>1 Large Onion, Finely Chopped</li><li>4 Cloves Garlic, Finely Chopped</li><li>2 Green Chillies, Finely Chopped</li><li> 1/4 Tsp Ground Turmeric</li><li> 1/2 Tsp Ground Cumin</li><li> 1/2 Cinnamon Stick, Broken Into Pieces</li><li>4 Cloves</li><li>Salt And Freshly Ground Black Pepper</li><li>1 Large Tomato, Chopped</li><li>1 Tsp Caster Sugar</li><li>Squeeze Lemon Juice</li><li>Small Handful Fresh Chopped Coriander</li></ul>

<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>Put the dal and mung beans in a medium saucepan and add the water, cover and bring to the boil. </li><li>Reduce heat to a simmer and cook for 50 mins stirring once to ensure a scum forms. </li><li>Pour out and discard the water, then return the dal to the pan. </li><li>Heat oil in a medium saucepan, add the onion, cloves and chillies and saute over low heat for ten minutes or until the onions are soft. </li><li>Add turmeric, cumin, cinnamon, cloves and salt and cook for 3 minutes or until the spices are fragrant and fragrant. </li><li>Add the onion mixture, tomato, sugar and mung bean to the dal and bring to the boil. </li><li>Reduce heat and simmer for 15 minutes, or until the dal is tender. </li><li>Remove the cinnamon stick pieces and add the lemon juice. </li><li>Remove from heat and blend for a few minutes until a smooth sauce has formed. </li><li>Add the coriander and serve. </li></ul>
