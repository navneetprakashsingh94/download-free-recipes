<h2>Ingredients With Quantity </h2>
<ul><li>55G/2Oz Fresh Suet, Finely Chopped, Or 85G/3Oz Shredded Suet</li><li>175G/6Oz Plain Flour, Plus Extra For Dusting</li><li>1 Tsp Salt</li><li> 1/2 Tsp Baking Powder</li><li>1 Tsp Horseradish Sauce (Optional)</li><li>1 Handful Fresh Parsley, Chopped (Optional)</li><li>Dash Milk Or Water</li><li>2Kg/4 1/2Lb Beef Brisket</li><li>3 Onions, Thickly Sliced</li><li>3 Carrots, Thickly Sliced</li><li>1 Celery Stick, Cut Into 2Cm/1In Pieces</li><li>350G/12Oz Potatoes, Cut Into Chunks</li><li>1 Bay Leaf</li><li>6 Sprigs Fresh Parsley</li><li>Water Or Beef Stock, To Cover The Meat</li></ul>

<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>Mix the suet with the flour, salt, baking powder and parsley. </li><li>Make a well in the center and make a well. </li><li>Pour the bbq sauce into it. </li><li>Mix and stir with a fork. </li><li>Add the milk. </li><li>Mix and knead with a fork. </li><li>Add more flour if necessary. </li><li>Let stand 20 min, covered, at room temperature. </li><li>Preheat oven to 350f. </li><li>Roll out the suet dough to about 1/4 inch thick. </li><li>Cut into 4 circles. </li><li>Put a heaping teaspoon of beef mixture into the centre of the circle. </li><li>Bring the corners up and down. </li><li>Pinch to seal. </li><li>Place dumplings onto a lightly floured baking sheet and brush the dumplings with the remaining bbq sauce. </li><li>Bake for 45 min to 1 hour, or until they are golden brown. </li><li>They are ready when they rise to the surface. </li><li>Serve with beef gravy. </li><li>In a large pan, place the meat and vegetables and cover with the meat stock. </li><li>Add bay leaf, parsley and water. </li><li>Bring to the boil, then reduce heat and simmer, covered, for 1 1/2 2 hrs, until meat and vegetables are tender. </li><li>Note a meat grinder works fine. </li><li>The meat will still hold its shape but must be ground rather than ground. </li><li>I recommend use this method to make stock a day in advance and keep it cool. </li><li>Use at room temperature. </li></ul>
