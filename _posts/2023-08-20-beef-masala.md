<h2>Ingredients With Quantity </h2>
<ul><li>1 Onion, Roughly Chopped</li><li>1 Tbsp Chopped Fresh Ginger</li><li>1 Tbsp Chopped Galangal (Or Another Tbsp Of The Above)</li><li>1 Tbsp Chopped Garlic</li><li>1 Lemongrass Stalk, Tough Outer Layer Removed And Roughly Chopped</li><li>1 Tsp Turmeric</li><li>6 Long Red Dried Chillies, Soaked In Water And Roughly Chopped</li><li>2 Tbsp Sunflower Oil</li><li>1 Cinnamon Stick</li><li>6 Cardamom Pods</li><li>650G/1Lb 7Oz Braising Steak, Cut Into 2Cm/1Inch Cubes</li><li>4 Makrut Lime Leaves</li><li>Zest Of A Lime</li><li>400Ml/14Fl Oz Coconut Milk</li><li>1 Tbsp Of Tamarind Paste Or Juice Of A Lime</li><li>50G/2Oz Desiccated Coconut Flakes</li><li>Coriander Sprigs To Garnish</li><li>1 Tbsp Sunflower Oil</li><li>1 Cinnamon Stick</li><li>4 Cardamom Pods</li><li>250G/9Oz Basmati Rice</li><li>600Ml/1Pint 1 1/2Fl Oz Water</li><li>2 Tbsp Chopped Fresh Coriander</li><li>Sea Salt</li><li>Deep-Fried Shallots</li><li>1 Deep-Fried Red Chilli</li></ul>

<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>Place all the ingredients in a large pot and make sure that you put the lid on top. </li><li>Cover and put on the fire. </li><li>Turn down the heat and leave to cook for a half hour. </li><li>If you have time, put the lid back on and if it is not, leave to cook for 10 more minutes. </li><li>Once cooked, taste and adjust the seasoning. </li><li>Heat the oil in a big, heavy pot and add the sliced onions and ginger. </li><li>Saute for a minute or two, then add the beef cubes and cook for a few minutes, turning them occasionally. </li><li>When they are lightly browned, add the makrut lime leaves and lime zest. </li><li>Once the mixture starts to bubble, add the coconut milk and bring to the boil. </li><li>Turn the heat down, and cook for 2 hours, stirring from time to time, until the meat is falling apart. </li><li>If the sauce becomes too thick, add some water. </li><li>Season to taste. </li><li>Turn the heat off and leave the curry to stay hot. </li><li>In a small saucepan, add the tamarind juice and simmer for a few minutes. </li><li>Add the lime juice and stir well. </li><li>Pour the puree on top of the curry, then garnish with the toasted coconut and coriander</li></ul>
