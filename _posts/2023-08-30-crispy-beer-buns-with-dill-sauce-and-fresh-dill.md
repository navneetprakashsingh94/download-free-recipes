<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>For the peas, soak the peas overnight in 3 cups of water with 2 tsp bicarbonate of soda. </li><li>Strain well and return to the pan. Bring to the boil and cook gently for 2 minutes until the peas are tender. </li><li>Drain and pass them through a sieve to remove the starch, then toss in a pan of hot water to stop the cooking. </li><li>Melt the butter and season with salt and pepper. </li><li>Put the flour, yeast, salt and sugar into a big bowl. Make a well in the centre. </li><li>Put the beer into the well and gradually add the vinegar, stirring to incorporate. </li><li>Place the mixture in a food processor and run on low speed until a soft dough has formed. </li><li>Place the dough on a floured surface and knead for 5 minutes, or until smooth and elastic. </li><li>Add more flour if necessary. </li><li>Place the dough in a plastic bag and leave in a warm place for 2 hours or until pliable. </li><li>Dust a rolling pin with flour and roll out the dough to a rectangle 5 x 10 inches. </li><li>Spread the butter onto the dough and turn it out onto the lined baking sheet. </li><li>Shape the pastry into a square. </li><li>Place the dough onto the top shelf and cover with another rectangle of baking parchment. </li><li>Leave to proof for 45 minutes, or until the pastry is doubled in size. </li><li>Heat the oil in a</li></ul>
