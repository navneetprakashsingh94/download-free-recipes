<h2>Ingredients With Quantity </h2>
<ul><li>150Ml/5Fl Oz Whole Milk</li><li> 1/4 Tsp Fine Sea Salt</li><li>75G/2 1/2Oz Caster Sugar</li><li>1 Medium Free-Range Egg</li><li>1 Orange, Zest Only</li><li>1 Unwaxed Lemon, Zest Only</li><li>600G/1Lb 5Oz Strong White Flour, Plus Extra For Dusting</li><li>2 Tsp Fast-Action Yeast</li><li>100G/3 1/2Oz Unsalted Butter, Softened</li><li>1 Tsp Rapeseed Oil, For Greasing</li><li>200G/7Oz Dried Morello Cherries</li><li>75G/2 1/2Oz Dark Belgian Chocolate Chips, Chilled In The Fridge</li><li>20 White Sugar Cubes, Halved</li><li>4 Tbsp Cherry Brandy</li><li>1 Large Free-Range Egg, Beaten, For Egg Wash</li><li>20 Coloured Glace Cherries, Cut In Half</li><li>100G/3 1/2Fl Oz Nibbed Sugar</li><li>1 Tsp Ground Cinnamon, For Dusting</li><li>50G/1 3/4Oz Good-Quality Plain Chocolate (70% Cocoa Solids)</li></ul>

<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>In a saucepan, heat the milk. Stir in the salt, sugar and egg and cook over low heat until it reaches 120 degrees celsius. Remove from heat, stir in the fruit, let cool. </li><li>In the bowl of a stand mixer, add half the flour with the yeast and cream with the butter. Add the sugar/egg mixture gradually, alternating with the remaining flour. Cover and allow to rise for 1 hour. </li><li>Line a baking sheet with baking parchment. Working with 2 pieces of dough at a time, roll them into ropes a little smaller than your beignets, and place them on the baking sheet with the sugar side up. Bake in a preheated oven at 220 degrees celcius for 12 15 minutes. Remove from oven and allow to cool. </li><li>Place morello cherries in a saucepan and bring to a boil with the chocolate chips, sugar cubes and cherry brandy. Let cool. </li><li>Roll out each of the pretzel strands in a shape of a log and brush with beaten egg. Place some cherries on each pretzel and place another one on top. Set aside and allow the pretzels to dry overnight in a warm place. </li><li>To make icing place sugar and cinnamon in a bowl. Stir in the cocoa and heat up to 190 degrees celsius in a saucepan. </li><li>Using a fork, dip the pretzels in the egg wash and then roll them in the cinnamon sugar. Let it dry. Store for a minute or two then serve. </li><li>Enjoy</li></ul>
