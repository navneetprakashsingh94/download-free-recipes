<h2>Ingredients With Quantity </h2>
<ul><li>2 Tbsp Plain Flour, Seasoned With Salt And Freshly Ground Black Pepper</li><li>1Kg/2Lb 3Oz Braising Steak, Cut Into 5Cm/2In Pieces</li><li>55G/2Oz Unsalted Butter</li><li>150G/5 1/2 Oz Smoked Streaky Bacon, Cut Into 1Cm/ 1/4In Dice</li><li>2 Onions, Peeled And Thinly Sliced</li><li>1 Sprig Thyme</li><li>1 Bay Leaf</li><li>650Ml/23Fl Oz Irish Stout, Such As Guinness</li><li>80G/3Oz Stoneless Prunes Cut In Half</li><li>80G/3Oz Pickled Walnuts, Sliced</li><li>4 Tsp Balsamic Vinegar</li><li>4 Tsp Soft Dark Brown Sugar</li><li>2 Tsp Dijon Mustard</li><li>500Ml/10 1/2Fl Oz Beef Stock</li><li>Salt And Freshly Ground Black Pepper</li><li>235G/8Oz Plain Flour</li><li>120G/4Oz Shredded Suet</li><li>80G/3Oz Fine White Breadcrumbs</li><li>2 Tsp Parsley, Chopped</li><li>2 Tsp Chives, Chopped</li><li>Salt And Freshly Ground White Pepper</li><li>500Ml/17Fl Oz Beef Stock</li><li>1Kg/2 1/4Lb Floury Potatoes, Peeled, Washed And Cut Into 2.5Cm/1In Chunks</li><li>Salt And Ground White Pepper</li><li>85G/3Oz Unsalted Butter</li><li>225Ml/8Fl Oz Semi-Skimmed Milk, Warmed</li></ul>

<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>Dredge the beef pieces in flour, the seasoned with salt pepper. </li><li>Melt half the butter in a casserole or dutch oven over medium heat. </li><li>Add the bacon, onions, thyme and bay leaf and cook for about 10 minutes, stirring occasionally. </li><li>Add the meat, guinness, prunes, walnuts, vinegar, brown sugar, mustard and beef stock. </li><li>Cook, covered, over medium heat for about 1 hour, or until the meat is tender. </li><li>In a food processor, combine the flour, suet, breadcrumbs, parsley and chives until the suet is finely chopped. </li><li>Add the beef stock and pulse briefly until a dough forms. </li><li>Turn out onto a floured board, form into a ball and cover. </li><li>Let rest for ten minutes. </li><li>Roll out to about half the thickness of a rectangle, and cut into strips. </li><li>Fry in butter for about 2 minutes, then set aside. </li><li>Bring the stock to boil in a saucepan and add the potatoes. </li><li>Season. </li><li>Simmer for about 20 minutes, until tender. </li><li>Stir in the walnuts and cook a further 5 minutes. </li><li>Remove from the heat and let stand for 10 minutes. </li><li>Season again. </li><li>Serve with the stew on the side. </li></ul>
