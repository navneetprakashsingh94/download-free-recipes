<h2>Ingredients With Quantity </h2>
<ul><li>2 Tbsp Olive Oil</li><li>300G/10 1/2Oz Minced Pork</li><li> 1/2 Onion, Very Finely Chopped</li><li>1 Carrot, Grated</li><li>Vegetable Oil, For Frying</li><li>1 Tsp Grated Nutmeg</li><li>2 Tbsp Finely Chopped Fresh Parsley</li><li>1 Lemon, Juice And Zest</li><li>Salt And Freshly Ground Black Pepper</li><li>50G/1 3/4Oz Butter</li><li>75G/2 3/4Oz Plain Flour</li><li>225Ml/8Fl Oz Full-Fat Milk, Warmed</li><li>2 Free-Range Eggs, Beaten</li><li>125G/4 1/2Oz Breadcrumbs</li></ul>

<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>Heat the oil, add the liver and saute. </li><li>Add the onion and carrot, saute for a few minutes, add the flour and stir over a moderate heat for a few minutes. </li><li>Add the milk and bring to the boil, stirring constantly. </li><li>Simmer until the liver is jiggly. </li><li>Mash a little of the liver in a pan with a pinch of salt and the lemon zest, cover with a lid, and leave to cool. </li><li>Grind the liver back into the pan, stir in the parsley, season with salt and pepper and keep warm. </li><li>Make dumplings in a pan, heat the oil, melt the butter, sprinkle in the flour and stir for 5 minutes over a low heat, stirring constantly to avoid lumps. </li><li>When the flour mixture has cooled, add the milk, eggs and breadcrumbs, mix well. </li><li>Knead lightly, then roll into balls the size of a golf ball, drop into the simmering stock or water, cover and cook for 20 minutes, or until tender. </li><li>When the dumplings are cooked, remove the lid, add the mashed potato, mash and ham balls. </li><li>Season to taste. </li></ul>
