<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>Preheat the oven to 220 c/425 f/gas 6. </li><li>Heat oil in a large pan and cook the steaks over high heat for 3 minutes per side. Remove and place in a deep baking dish and wrap with foil, roasting for 20 minutes or until cooked to medium 160c/350f/gas mark 5. </li><li>Meanwhile, make the pastry brush the egg wash over the pastry to avoid spills. Roll out the pastry to a 3mm/1/4 in thickness and cut out six 4cm/1in squares using a sharp knife. </li><li>Place the pastry squares, one side at a time on a baking tray lined with non stick baking paper. Brush with the egg wash and bake for 25 minutes or until crisp and golden. </li><li>Meanwhile, melt the butter in a small frying pan over high heat. Fry the mushrooms for 3 4 minutes until tender. Add the mushroom mixture, parmesan, tarragon and mustard and mix well. Season with salt and pepper and set aside. </li><li>Meanwhile, preheat the oven to 200 c / 425 f. Brush both sides of the beef with beaten egg yolk and bake for 5 8 minutes, or until cooked to your liking. Remove from the oven and loosely cover with foil. Allow to rest for 10 minutes before slicing. </li><li>Meanwhile, make the mushroom sauce melt the butter in a frying pan over a low heat, add the mushrooms and cook for 5 minutes until softened.
