<h2>Ingredients With Quantity </h2>
<ul><li>3 Tbsp Vegetable Oil</li><li>Large Pinch Ground Asafoetida</li><li>1 Bay Leaf</li><li> 1/2 Tsp Panch Phoran (A Blend Of Equal Quantities Of Fenugreek, Nigella Seeds, Fennel Seeds, Cumin And Mustard Seeds), Available From Asian Supermarkets, Also Known As Bengali Five-Spice</li><li>1-2 Mild Dried Red Chillies</li><li>1 Small Onion, Chopped</li><li> 1/2 Tsp Ground Turmeric</li><li>2 Level Tsp Ground Cumin</li><li>1 Heaped Tsp Ground Coriander</li><li>2 Tsp Ginger Paste (Available From Asian Supermarkets, Or Make Your Own By Crushing Or Blending Fresh Ginger To A Rough Paste, Adding Water If Necessary To Loosen)</li><li>500G/1Lb 2Oz Butternut Squash, Peeled, Seeds Removed, Flesh Cut Into 4Cm/1 1/2In Cubes</li><li>200Ml/7Fl Oz Boiling Water</li><li>200G/7Oz Canned Chickpeas, Drained And Washed</li><li>1 Tsp Ground Garam Masala</li><li>1 Tsp Ground Fennel Seeds</li><li>Salt, To Taste</li><li>Sugar, To Taste</li></ul>

<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>Heat oil, when it bubbles, add the asafoetida. Let it crackle and then add the bay leaf, and the fenugreek nigella seeds and cook until fragrant. </li><li>Add the ginger paste and water, then add the chopped onions. Cook till they turn golden. </li><li>Add the fenugreek, ground fennel seeds, and all the ground spices and stir fry for two minutes. </li><li>Finally add the butternut squash cubes, and stir fry for three minutes. </li><li>Add the chickpeas, and let it simmer for one hour until the mixture comes together. </li><li>Put in the ground cumin, cumin, salt and sugar, then let simmer for one hour. </li><li>Turn off the heat and leave it to stand for at least 10 minutes. </li><li>Serve hot with steamed rice. </li></ul>
