<h2>Ingredients With Quantity </h2>
<ul><li>50G/1 3/4Oz Lightly Salted Butter, Softened</li><li>50G/1 3/4Oz Caster Sugar</li><li>1 Loaf Medium Sliced White Bread, Crusts Removed</li><li>300G/10 1/2Oz Caster Sugar</li><li>100G/3 1/2Oz Lightly Salted Butter</li><li>2Kg/4Lb 8Oz Cox Apples, Peeled, Cored And Cut Into 8 Equal Wedges</li><li>500Ml/18Fl Oz Full-Fat Milk</li><li>1 Vanilla Pod, Seeds Scraped</li><li>5 Free-Range Egg Yolks</li><li>30G/1Oz Caster Sugar</li></ul>

<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>Preheat oven to 180 c approximately 400 f or gas mark 6. Line a 4. 5 x 3. 5 loaf tin with baking paper. </li><li>To make the caramelized apples, combine sugar and butter in a medium saucepan over a medium heat. Stir constantly for about 10 minutes. Add apples and stir until caramelised. Remove pan from heat. Spoon apples into prepared tin and place in a larger oven dish. </li><li>To make the pudding, place milk, vanilla seeds and milk in a large saucepan over a medium heat. Remove from heat, cover and leave for 10 minutes to infuse flavour. </li><li>Place egg yolks and sugar in a medium bowl and beat vigorously with a wire whisk for about 1 minute or until light and frothy. </li><li>Add half the hot milk to egg mixture and stir until well combined. Return mixture to saucepan and cook over medium heat for 15 20 minutes or until a custard forms. Remove from heat. </li><li>Gently combine bread and apple custard in a large bowl. Transfer to prepared tin. </li><li>Bake pudding for 30 minutes or until set and golden brown. </li><li>Serve pudding with caramelized apple. </li></ul>
