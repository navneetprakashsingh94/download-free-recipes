<h2>Ingredients With Quantity </h2>
<ul><li>375G/13Oz Dried Mixed Fruit</li><li>1 Medium Orange, Zest And Juice</li><li>1 Heaped Tsp Ground Mixed Spice</li><li>25G/1Oz Clear Honey</li><li>225Ml/8Fl Oz Earl Grey Tea, Hot</li><li>Unsalted Butter, For Greasing</li><li>1 Medium Free-Range Egg, Beaten</li><li>1 Large Eating Apple (Approximately 200G/7Oz), Cored, Grated</li><li>175G/6Oz Wholemeal Self-Raising Flour, Sifted</li><li>200G/7Oz White Self-Raising Flour, Sifted</li></ul>

<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>Put the fruit into a small saucepan, reserving the juice</li><li>Add the apricots, zest, juice, spice, honey and tea and bring to the boil. </li><li>Simmer for 5 mins, remove from heat and set aside to cool slightly. </li><li>Rub the butter into the flours, then add in the egg and apple. </li><li>Mix to a smooth batter</li><li>Spoon into an oiled 5. 5 x 10. 5cm tin</li><li>Smooth the surface and place into the freezer for about 2 hours. </li><li>When ready to bake, preheat the oven to 180c or 180c fan forced. </li><li>Brush the surface with some of the cooled apricot syrup, then bake for about 45 mins. </li><li>Check that the cake isn t browning too quickly if so, use a fork</li><li>Leave to stand for a few min and turn out of the tin onto a wire rack</li><li>This gives a little flavour</li><li>Tip on the tin as soon as it comes out of the oven. </li><li>It will swell to about 5 cm, then fall slightly</li><li>Let it cool completely before removing from the tin</li><li>You can keep it in the fridge for a long time. </li></ul>
