<h2>Ingredients With Quantity </h2>
<ul><li>150G/5 1/2Oz Chapatti Or Wholemeal Flour, Plus Extra For Dusting</li><li>1 Tsp Salt</li><li>2-3 Tbsp Ghee, For Frying</li><li>Mint Yoghurt, Chutney Or Salad, To Serve</li><li>4 Free-Range Eggs, Beaten</li><li> 1/2 Red Onion, Finely Chopped</li><li>1 Garlic Clove, Crushed</li><li>5-6 Cherry Tomatoes, Roughly Chopped</li><li>4 Tbsp Finely Chopped Fresh Coriander</li><li>2 Tsp Cumin Seeds, Toasted</li></ul>

<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>To make the dough, sift together the flour and salt. </li><li>Rub in the ghee until the mix resembles fine breadcrumbs. </li><li>Make a well in the centre and slowly add the egg a little at a time mixing well until the dough leaves the sides of the bowl and forms a ball. </li><li>Wrap it in plastic wrap and leave it in the refrigerator for about 1 hour. </li><li>For the filling, fry the onion in a little oil until soft, then add the clove and tomatoes, stir and cook for 10 minutes. </li><li>Add the coriander and cumin seeds, stir again, cover and leave it in the fridge. </li><li>Heat the oven to 180c. </li><li>Divide the dough in half, and roll out into 4 circles. </li><li>Put a generous spoonful of filling onto the centre of the circle. </li><li>Fold the dough over and seal. </li><li>Place the naan on a baking tray lined with baking paper. </li><li>Prick the top with a fork to allow steam to escape, then bake in the oven for about 20 minutes, or until cooked. </li><li>Serve the naan with mint or yoghurt, if you like. </li></ul>
