<h2>Ingredients With Quantity </h2>
<ul><li>225G/8Oz Plain Flour</li><li>8 Free-Range Eggs</li><li>570Ml/1 Pint Milk</li><li>1 Tbsp Olive Oil</li><li>6 Beef Sausages</li><li>75G/3Oz Beef Dripping</li><li>2 Sprigs Fresh Thyme, Leaves Only</li><li>Salt And Freshly Ground Black Pepper</li><li>50G/2Oz Butter</li><li>2 Onions, Sliced</li><li>1 Tbsp Instant Gravy Granules</li><li>1 Tbsp Yeast Extract</li><li>400Ml/14Fl Oz Vegetable Stock</li><li>Salt And Freshly Ground Black Pepper</li></ul>

<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>Beat the eggs with the milk, then stir in the oil</li><li>Sift the flour into a large mixing bowl, add the wet mixture, kneading it well with your fingers and knead it well for 2 3 minutes</li><li>Shape a ball and put in a bowl, cover with a cloth and leave in a warm spot for 1 hour to rise</li><li></li><li>Slice the meat and sausage into rounds using a cleaver and press down to flatten some</li><li></li><li>Fry sausages and meat in batches on both sides in a little oil until it browns and browned all over, then remove with a slotted spoon</li><li>Add the onions to the pan and cook until softened</li><li>Add the gravy granules, yeast extract and stock, simmer for 3 4 minutes then return the sausages to the pan along with the beef</li><li>Season the gravy with pepper and transfer to a casserole dish</li><li></li><li>Preheat the oven to 220c and line the bottom of the tin with grease proof paper</li><li>Spoon the gyro blinis into the tin. </li><li>Bake for 25 30 minutes, until the gyro blinis have set on top and are golden. </li><li></li><li>Serve with salad and bread</li></ul>
