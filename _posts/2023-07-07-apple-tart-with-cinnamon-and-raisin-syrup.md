<h2>Ingredients With Quantity </h2>
<ul><li>50G/1 3/4Oz Raisins</li><li>1 Green Cooking Apple, Halved And Core Removed (Not Peeled)</li><li>3 Red Eating Apples, Halved And Cores Removed (Not Peeled)</li><li>40G/1 1/2Oz Unsalted Butter, Diced</li><li>50G/1 3/4Oz Caster Sugar</li><li>5G/ 1/8 Oz Ground Cinnamon</li><li>1 X 320G/11 1/2Oz Puff Pastry Sheet</li><li>50Ml/2Fl Oz Calvados</li><li>3 Free-Range Eggs, 2 Yolks And 1 Whole, Beaten</li><li>10G/ 1/3 Oz Caster Sugar</li><li>Pinch Ground Cinnamon</li></ul>

<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>Place the raisins in a bowl with a little bit of water for 30 minutes and mash with the back of a wooden spoon. </li><li>Saute the apple chunks with the butter, sugar, and cinnamon in a pan with a little water. </li><li>Add the fruit to the cooked and stir through for a few minutes. </li><li>Allow the fruit to cool. </li><li>Preheat the oven to 190c. </li><li>Roll out the puff pastry on a lightly floured surface, and roll out the pastry to a rectangle about 1cm thick. </li><li>Cover the fruit with the pastry. </li><li>Pour in the port and fill the pastry with the fruit mixture, overlapping the pieces to create a rim around the edge. </li><li>Beat the egg yolks in a small bowl, add the caster sugar and a pinch of cinnamon and mix well. </li><li>Brush the pastry with egg and sprinkle the remaining sugar and cinnamon on top. </li><li>Bake in the oven for 35 40 minutes, or until cooked. </li><li>Serve the tart with vanilla ice cream or custard. </li></ul>
