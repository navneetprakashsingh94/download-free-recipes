<h2>Ingredients With Quantity </h2>
<ul><li>300Ml/10 1/2Fl Oz Full-Fat Milk</li><li>200Ml/7Fl Oz Double Cream</li><li>75G/2 1/2Oz Caster Sugar</li><li>6 Free-Range Egg Yolks</li><li>3 Chocolate And Peanut Bars, Chopped</li><li>200G/7Oz Plain Flour, Plus Extra For Dusting</li><li>150G/5 1/2Oz Unsalted Butter, Chopped</li><li>75G/2 1/2Oz Caster Sugar</li><li>2 Free-Range Egg Yolks</li><li>400G/14Oz Fresh Blueberries</li><li>1 Vanilla Pod, Split In Half Lengthways, Seeds Scraped Out</li><li>2 Tbsp Cornflour</li><li>1 Lemon, Juice Only</li><li>2 Tbsp Demerara Sugar</li></ul>

<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>In a saucepan, heat milk and cream until bubbles form around the edge. Mix the caster sugar and egg yolks to a pale, and then slowly pour the hot milk mixture into the egg yolks while whisking constantly. Strain it into the saucepan and cook gently on a low heat, stirring frequently, until thickened. </li><li>Chop the chocolate peanut bars. Mix the flour, butter and sugar together and mix it into the pastry base with your hands. Wrap the pastry in plastic film and chill it for 2 3 hours. </li><li>Heat oven to 200 degrees celsius. Line the base of a pie dish with foil and fill with pie weights. Cook for 35 minutes. </li><li>Make the meringue beat the egg yolks with the lemon juice and the vanilla extract in a bowl. Mix the demerara sugar and vanilla essence together to make a thick paste. Once the pastry has risen, leave it in the refrigerator for 20 minutes. </li><li>Spread the blueberry custard in the pie dish and sprinkle the meringue on top. Bake for 35 40 minutes until the meringue is golden. </li></ul>
