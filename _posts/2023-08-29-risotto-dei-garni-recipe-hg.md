<h2>Ingredients With Quantity </h2>
<ul><li>50G/1 3/4Oz Butter</li><li>50G/1 3/4Oz Fresh Parsley, Chopped</li><li> 1/2 Garlic Clove, Crushed</li><li>4 Banana Shallots, Three Halved (With Skin On) And One Finely Chopped</li><li>2 Garlic Cloves, Finely Chopped</li><li>50G/1 3/4Oz Arborio Rice</li><li>50Ml/2Fl Oz White Wine</li><li>300Ml/10Fl Oz Chicken Stock</li><li>25Ml/1Fl Oz Olive Oil</li><li>1 X 500G/1Lb 2Oz Beef Fillet (Preferably Dexter Beef)</li><li>Salt And Freshly Ground Black Pepper</li><li>2 Tbsp Mascarpone</li><li>50G/1 3/4Oz Parmesan, Grated</li><li>1 Small Bunch Fresh Parsley, Leaves Chopped</li></ul>

<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>Place the butter, parsley and clove on a saucepan. </li><li>Cook over a low heat for 2 3 minutes until the butter is just melted and the parsley is just lightly browned. </li><li>Set aside. </li><li>Set the aside. </li><li>In the same saucepan, heat the oil and fry the shallot and chopped garlic. </li><li>When it has softened add the arborio rice and stir well. </li><li>After a couple of minutes, add the wine. </li><li>Cook until most of the liquid has been absorbed. </li><li>Continue adding the wine in stages, stirring every 30 seconds until it is completely absorbed. </li><li>Stir in the chicken stock and add a little more stock. </li><li>When the stock has been absorbed add the remaining liquid, stirring in gently with a ladle, while adding stock, stirring and allowing the liquid to absorb before adding more. </li><li>When the rice is soft, but still holds its shape, add the chopped shallots. </li><li>Stir over a medium heat for a couple of minutes then add the chopped beef, salt and pepper. </li><li>Cook over a high heat for 15 20 minutes. </li><li>Remove from the heat and add the mascarpone, parmesan and chopped parsley. </li><li>Stir well and serve immediately. </li></ul>
