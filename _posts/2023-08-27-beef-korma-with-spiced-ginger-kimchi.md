<h2>Ingredients With Quantity </h2>
<ul><li>5 Shallots, Chopped</li><li>Knob Fresh Root Ginger Or Galangal, Peeled And Chopped</li><li>2 Lemongrass Stalks, Chopped</li><li>6 Garlic Cloves, Chopped</li><li>10 Dried Chillies, Rehydrated In Warm Water</li><li>4 Tbsp Vegetable Oil</li><li>1 Cinnamon Stick</li><li>2 Star Anise</li><li>4 Cardamom Pods, Smashed</li><li>3 Cloves</li><li>2 Lemongrass Stalks, Smashed</li><li>1Kg/2Lb 4Oz Beef Shin, Diced</li><li>400Ml Tin Coconut Milk</li><li>200G/7Oz Plain Yoghurt</li><li>1 Tbsp Tamarind Paste</li><li>6 Kaffir Lime Leaves, Shredded</li><li>50G/1 3/4Oz Palm Sugar</li><li>50G/1 3/4Oz Coconut Flakes, Toasted</li><li> 1/2 Cucumber, Chopped</li><li>1 Red Chilli, Chopped</li></ul>

<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>For the spice paste, place shallots, ginger, lemongrass, cloves and chillies in a large heatproof bowl. Cover with water and place over a pan of simmering water. Steam, covered, for 5 mins or until shallots are soft. Remove the shallots and lemongrass and set aside. Remove and discard the spice paste. Add oil to a large deep saucepan. Heat on medium high heat. Add cinnamon, star anise, cardamom, cloves and lemongrass. Cook, stirring, for 2 mins or until fragrant. </li><li>Add shin cubes and cook, stirring frequently, for 2 mins or until well browned. Add the spice paste and cook, stirring, for 3 mins or until fragrant. Return the shallots and lemongrass to the pan and add the coconut milk. Bring to a boil. Reduce heat to low and simmer for 3 hours or until beef is tender. </li><li>Meanwhile, whisk yoghurt, tamarind paste, lime leaves and sugar into beef mixture. Cook, stirring, for 5 mins or until sauce thickens slightly. </li><li>Serve beef in a shallow serving dish topped with sauce. Sprinkle with coconut and garnish with cucumber and chilli. </li></ul>
