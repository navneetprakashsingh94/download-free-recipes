<h2>Ingredients With Quantity </h2>
<ul><li>1 Pasilla Chilli, Seeds Removed</li><li>100G/3 1/2Oz Salted Butter</li><li>1 Large Red Onion, Diced</li><li>3 Garlic Cloves, Chopped</li><li>20G/ 1/2Oz Achiote Paste, Softened (Avaliable At Specialist Stores And Online)</li><li>2 Tbsp Orange Juice</li><li>2 Poblano Chillies, Deseeded And Finely Sliced (Or Alternatively 3-4 Tbsp Pickled Jalapeno Peppers)</li><li>3 Large Tomatoes, Deseeded And Roughly Chopped</li><li>250G/9Oz Fresh White Crab Meat</li><li>Small Handful Fresh Coriander, Roughly Chopped</li><li> 1/2 Tsp Salt</li><li>30G/1Oz Panko Breadcrumbs</li><li>30G/1Oz Parmesan (Or Alternaive Vegetarian Hard Cheese), Grated</li></ul>

<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>Toast the chilli seeds in a skillet. Remove from heat and set aside to cool</li><li>Heat the butter in a skillet and fry the onion until softened. Add the ground chilli seeds and achiote paste. Let the paste ooze out of the pan. Then add the orange juice. Taste for balance of seasoning and add salt if needed. </li><li>In a fry pan, cook the chilis, tomatoes, and orange juice for 4 5 minutes, breaking up the tomatoes with a wooden spoon. Remove from heat and let cool. </li><li>Mix all ingredients well, adding the crabmeat last. The crabmeat is easier to mix if it s done in advance. </li><li>Preheat your grill/broiler. </li><li>When the grill/broiler reaches 550 f, fill the chilies with the crabmeat mixture and tuck the tops under. Brush with butter or olive oil and grill for 3 5 minutes on each side until browned and crispy. Serve with a grilled egg. </li></ul>
