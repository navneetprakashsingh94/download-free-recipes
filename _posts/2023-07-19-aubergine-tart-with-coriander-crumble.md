<h2>Ingredients With Quantity </h2>
<ul><li>1 Large Or 2 Medium Aubergines</li><li>1 Tbsp Sunflower Oil</li><li>1 Tsp Mustard Seeds</li><li>1 Red Onion, Finely Chopped</li><li>2 1/2Cm/1In Piece Fresh Root Ginger, Finely Chopped</li><li>2 Tbsp Sundried Tomato Paste</li><li> 1/2 Tsp Salt</li><li>1 Green Chilli, Finely Chopped</li><li> 1/2 Tsp Caster Sugar</li><li>Bunch Of Fresh Coriander, Finely Chopped</li><li>Handful Red Amaranth Micro Herbs (Optional)</li><li>60G/2 1/4Oz Unsalted Butter</li><li> 1/4 Tsp Salt</li><li>Handful Coriander Leaves, Finely Chopped</li><li>85G/3Oz Plain Flour</li><li>2 Large Free-Range Eggs, Beaten</li></ul>

<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>Preheat oven to 180c/gas 4. </li><li>Roast aubergine for 30 40 minutes, turning them every 10 15 minutes, until tender. </li><li>Drain, pat dry and place in a shallow baking pan. </li><li>Toss in a little oil to lightly coat. </li><li>Bake for 15 minutes. </li><li>The aubergine will puff up and get crispy on top. </li><li>You can also make it in a regular oven. </li><li>Heat 1 tbsp oil in a large frying pan, add the mustard seeds and stir for a minute. </li><li>Add the onion and ginger and fry until softened. </li><li>Mix the paste, salt and chilli into the pan. </li><li>Cook for 1 minute, stirring. </li><li>Add the sugar, mix well, and cook for 2 minutes, stirring. </li><li>Add the coriander and micro herbs and mix well. </li><li>Remove from the heat. </li><li>Let cool. </li><li>Melt the butter and 1 tsp salt in a food processor. </li><li>Add the herbs, and process until finely chopped. </li><li>Transfer to a bowl and stir in the flour and eggs. </li><li>Season with 1 tsp salt. </li><li>Knead gently until smooth. </li><li>Roll out between 2 sheets of parchment paper to 1/16 inch thick. </li><li>Transfer the pastry to a non stick bakng sheet and press with your fingers to a thickness of 1. </li><li>Trim off excess pastry. </li><li>Roll out the pastry to a thickness of 1/8 inch. </li><li>Fit into a 22cm/22cm round tart tin, lining the sides slightly and up the sides. </li><li>Prick</li></ul>
