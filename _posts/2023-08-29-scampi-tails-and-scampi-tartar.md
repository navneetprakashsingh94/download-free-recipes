<h2>Ingredients With Quantity </h2>
<ul><li>16-20 Wild Scottish Scampi Tails, Peeled And Thawed (If Frozen)</li><li>4 Tbsp Plain Flour</li><li> 1/2 Tsp Fine Sea Salt</li><li>Lemon Wedges, To Serve</li><li>75G/3Oz Cornflour</li><li>200G/7Oz Plain Flour</li><li>1 Tsp Fine Sea Salt</li><li>330Ml/12Fl 0Z Bottle Of Real Ale</li><li>2 Tbsp White Wine Vinegar</li><li>2 Free-Range Egg Yolks</li><li>1 Tbsp White Wine Vinegar</li><li>2 Tsp Dijon Mustard</li><li> 1/4 Tsp Caster</li><li>Pinch Fine Sea Salt</li><li>150Ml/5Fl Oz Sunflower Oil</li><li>50Ml/2Fl Oz Extra Virgin Olive Oil</li><li>50G/2Oz Capers, Drained And Roughly Chopped</li><li>50G/2Oz Mini Gherkins (Cornichons), Drained And Thinly Sliced</li><li>2 Tbsp Chopped Fresh Parsley</li><li>1 Tbsp Chopped Fresh Tarragon</li></ul>

<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>Heat oven to 450f. </li><li>Mix together the cornflour, flour and salt. </li><li>Add the ale and vinegar and whisk all the time until the batter becomes smooth. </li><li>Pour it into a shallow dish and dredge each scampi tail in the batter, coating all sides. </li><li>Bake the tails until golden and cooked through, 12 15 minutes, checking at 10 minute intervals and turning once halfway through. </li><li>Make the tartar sauce in a food processor, combine the yolks, vinegar, mustard and sugar and whisk until pale. </li><li>Slowly add the oil until the mixture is thick and creamy. </li><li>Whisk in the capers, gherkins, parsley and tarragon. </li><li>Season with salt. </li><li>Make the tartar sauce in a food processor, combine the scampi tails with the prepared sauce and reheat gently on the stove. </li><li>Remove the scampi tails from the oven and serve warm, with the lemon wedges. </li></ul>
