<h2>Ingredients With Quantity </h2>
<ul><li>Salt Cod Skin (See Below)</li><li>150G/5 1/2Oz Dried Chickpeas, Soaked Overnight In Cold Water And Drained</li><li>150G/5 1/2Oz Dried White Beans, Soaked Overnight In Cold Water And Drained</li><li>1 Garlic Bulb, Cloves Peeled</li><li>1 Bay Leaf</li><li>1 Small Leek, Roughly Chopped</li><li>2 Tbsp Extra Virgin Olive Oil</li><li>1 Onion, Finely Chopped</li><li>1 Tsp Pimenton De La Vera Dulce (Sweet Spanish Smoked Paprika)</li><li>4 Free-Range Eggs</li><li>2 Large Handfuls Spinach</li><li>Sea Salt And Freshly Ground Black Pepper</li><li>250G/9Oz Stale Bread, Crusts Removed</li><li>150Ml/ 1/4 Pint Full-Fat Milk</li><li>250G/9Oz Salt Cod, Soaked For 24-48 Hours In Cold Water, (Changing The Water Every 12 Hours)Drained, Skin Removed And Reserved</li><li>Large Bunch Flatleaf Parsley, Finely Chopped</li><li>Olive Oil, For Frying</li><li>Freshly Ground Pepper</li></ul>

<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>Preheat the oven to 220 c or 350 f. </li><li>For the chickpeas</li><li>Soak the chickpeas in water for a minimum of 8 hours. Drain. Wash the chickpeas under running water and set aside. </li><li>Bring the chickpeas, water, cloves, bay leaf and leek to the boil. </li><li>Reduce the heat to low and cook for 1 hour or until the chickpeas are tender. Remove the leek from the water. Allow the chickpeas to cool in the liquid. When cool, peel the skin and discard the core. </li><li>To make the stuffing, heat the oil in a non stick pan and add the onion and pimenton. Cook until soft. Add the spinach, cook for 1 minute, remove from the pan and add the eggs, season and mix well. </li><li>Put the bread in a food processor, add the milk and process until smooth. Season with pepper and cut the cod into pieces. </li><li>Spread the chickpeas in an ovenproof dish. Add the spinach and the chickpeas. Add the eggs, sprinkle with the parsley leaves and then pour over the fish mixture. </li><li>Cover with tin foil and bake for 40 minutes. Remove foil, cut into slices and serve hot. </li></ul>
