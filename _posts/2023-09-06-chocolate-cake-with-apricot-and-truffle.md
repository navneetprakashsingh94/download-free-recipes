<h2>Ingredients With Quantity </h2>
<ul><li>100G/3 1/2Oz Baking Spread Or Soft Butter, Plus Extra For Greasing</li><li>50G/1 3/4Oz Cocoa Powder</li><li>6 Tbsp Boiling Water</li><li>4 Free-Range Eggs</li><li>175G/6Oz Self-Raising Flour</li><li>1 Tsp Baking Powder</li><li>250G/9Oz Caster Sugar</li><li>300Ml/10Fl Oz Pouring Double Cream</li><li>300G/10 1/2Oz Dark Chocolate, Broken Into Small Pieces</li><li>3 Tbsp Apricot Jam</li><li>5 White Chocolate Truffle Balls</li><li>3 Large Strawberries, Halved, Green Stalk Left Attached</li><li>25G/1Oz Dark Chocolate, Melted</li></ul>

<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>Preheat the oven to 180 degrees celsius. </li><li>Heat the butter, cocoa powder, and boiling water in a saucepan over medium heat. </li><li>When the mixture is bubbling, remove from the heat and allow to cool. </li><li>In a bowl, whisk the eggs, one at a time, making sure to incorporate each egg before adding the next. </li><li>Sift the flour and baking powder together, then gradually add the sugar. </li><li>Pour into the cocoa mix, and beat well. </li><li>Pour into the cake tins and bake for 15 to 20 minutes. </li><li>Let cool and spread each cooled cake with the apricot cream and shave chocolate. </li><li>For the glaze, heat the apricot jam and pour over the cake. </li><li>Place the cake in the refrigerator for about 30 minutes or until it starts to set. </li><li>Place on a serving dish and garnish with truffle balls and strawberries, if desired. </li><li>Melt the chocolate and drizzle the cream over the strawberries and serve. </li></ul>
