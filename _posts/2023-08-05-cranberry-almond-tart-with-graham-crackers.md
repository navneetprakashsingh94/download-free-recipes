<h2>Ingredients With Quantity </h2>
<ul><li>300G/10 1/2Oz Plain Flour, Plus Extra For Dusting</li><li>Big Pinch Salt</li><li>150G/5 1/2Oz Cold Butter, Cubed</li><li>100G/3 1/2Oz Butter</li><li>100G/3 1/2Oz Caster Sugar</li><li>100G/3 1/2Oz Ground Almonds</li><li>25G/1Oz Plain Flour</li><li>2 Free-Range Eggs, Beaten</li><li>6 Tbsp Cranberry Compote (Or Good-Quality Cranberry Sauce Without Vinegar)</li><li>40G/1 1/2Oz Flaked Almonds</li><li>80G/3Oz Icing Sugar</li></ul>

<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees. Grease and line a 9 inch tin, line with grease proof paper, then fill it with dried beans and bake in preheated oven for 20 minutes or until lightly golden. </li><li></li><li>2. In a bowl, mix together the flour, sugar and salt. With your fingers, rub in the butter or margarine until the mixture resembles fine breadcrumbs. Add the egg yolks and stir to combine well. Add the flour and mix again, then gently fold in the egg whites until the mixture is completely blended and smooth. </li><li></li><li>3. In another bowl beat the egg whites until they form soft peaks. Fold in the streusel. Spoon the mixture evenly into the pie crust and bake for 25 minutes or until the bottom is golden. Remove from oven and cool on a rack. Slice and serve with cranberry sauce and slivered almonds. </li><li></li><li>2. To make the almonds, melt the butter in a small saucepan or the microwave. Set aside to cool. In a bowl, mix together the almonds and caster sugar. Spread the almond mixture on top of the cooled tart, leaving a 1 inch border all around. Place in the refrigerator or freezer and chill for 1 hour, until set. Sprinkle with icing sugar before serving. </li><li></li><li>If using a non stick pan, heat the butter for a minute on a low heat and add the sugar. Cook on medium heat until it caramelises and then pour onto the chilled tart. Allow the tart to cool for 20 minutes then remove the paper from the tart. </li></ul>
