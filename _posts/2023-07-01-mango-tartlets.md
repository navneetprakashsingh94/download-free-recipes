<h2>Ingredients With Quantity </h2>
<ul><li>200G/7Oz Plain Flour, Plus Extra For Dusting</li><li>25G/1Oz Light Brown Caster Sugar</li><li>4-5 Green Cardamom Pods, Seeds Removed And Crushed</li><li>125G/4 1/2Oz Cold Unsalted Butter, Grated</li><li>1 Free-Range Egg Yolk, Beaten</li><li>4 Large Free-Range Eggs</li><li>175G/6Oz Caster Sugar</li><li>175Ml/6Fl Oz Double Cream</li><li>175G/6Oz Tinned Alphonso Mango Pulp</li></ul>

<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>To make the pastry, combine the flour, sugar and cardamom, and cut the butter into the flour until you ve created pea size balls. Add the egg yolk and mix well with your hands. You should have a crumbly dough, do not add any cold water or more sugar as it will affect the thickness and texture. Shape into 2 inch diameter balls, wrap in plastic wrap and refrigerate for 1 hour. </li><li>Roll out each ball of pastry on a lightly floured work surface to a 10 inch round. Using a 3 inch pastry cutter, cut out 12 rounds from each disk. Lightly flour the surface of each and roll out each pastry round to 2 1/2 inch round. Transfer to a sheet tray lined with parchment paper and refrigerate for 30 minutes. </li><li>For the filling, beat the eggs, sugar and 3 tbsp cold water in a bowl. Mix until thick and pale. Fold in the cream and mango pulp, then pour into 12 lined 3 inch tartlet pans. Refrigerate for 30 minutes. </li><li>Preheat the oven to 375 f. Remove the tartlets from the freezer and place them on a wire rack to cool. Remove the paper from the pastry cases and arrange the pastry cases on a baking tray. Bake for 10 mins. Reduce the oven temperature to 325 f and bake the tartlets for 40 mins, until lightly browned. Cool for 10 mins before removing from the tartlets. Cool completely on a wire rack. </li></ul>
