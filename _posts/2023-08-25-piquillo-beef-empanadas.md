<h2>Ingredients With Quantity </h2>
<ul><li>450G/1Lb Plain Flour, Plus Extra For Dusting</li><li>2 Tsp Baking Powder</li><li>1 Tsp Salt</li><li>60G/2 1/4Oz Butter</li><li>60G/2 1/4Oz Lard Or Vegetable Shortening</li><li>75-100Ml/2 1/2-3 1/2Fl Oz Chilled Water</li><li>2 Tbsp Olive Oil</li><li> 1/2 Beef Stock Cube, Crumbled</li><li>400G/14Oz Fillet Or Sirloin Steak, Finely Chopped</li><li>1 Onion, Finely Chopped</li><li>1 Small Green Pepper, Finely Chopped</li><li>3 Garlic Cloves, Crushed</li><li>3 Tbsp Red Wine</li><li> 1/2 Tsp Ground Cumin</li><li> 1/2 Tsp Paprika</li><li> 1/2 Tsp Chilli Powder</li><li>12 Pimento-Stuffed Green Olives, Sliced</li><li>1 Tsp Salt</li><li> 1/2 Tsp Ground Black Pepper</li><li>1 Beaten Free-Range Egg, For Brushing Pastry</li></ul>

<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>1. Mix the flour, baking powder, and salt together. Cut the butter and lard into the dry ingredients until the texture looks crumbly. Make a well in the center and add the water and mix well with your fingertips until the dough is evenly moistened. Chill the dough for at least 30 minutes. </li><li></li><li>2. Heat the oil in a frying pan and add the beef cube, stirring to break up the pieces. Cook for about 5 minutes or until the beef is lightly browned. </li><li></li><li>3. Add the onions, poblano pepper, and cloves and cook for another 5 minutes. Add the wine and simmer until the wine is reduced by half. Add the cumin, paprika, chilli powder and olives and continue cooking for another 5 minutes. Add salt and pepper to taste. Set aside. </li><li></li><li>4. Remove the pastry from the refrigerator and roll out out to 1/8 inch thickness using a 3 1/2 inch round cookie cutter. Spread the beef filling over the dough and top with a rolled out disk. </li><li></li><li>5. Cut into quarters and arrange on a lightly greased baking sheet. Brush the tops with egg beaten with a little water and bake at 375 degrees for 15 minutes or until golden. Serve hot. </li></ul>
