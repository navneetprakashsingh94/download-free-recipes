<h2>Ingredients With Quantity </h2>
<ul><li> 1/4 White Cabbage, Shredded</li><li>1 Carrot, Peeled And Shredded</li><li> 1/2 Red Onion, Finely Sliced</li><li>2 Heaped Tbsp Vegan Yoghurt Or Vegan Mayonnaise</li><li>1 Tsp Mustard Seeds</li><li>2 Bananas</li><li>1 Tbsp Ground Turmeric</li><li>1 Tbsp Vegetable Oil</li><li> 1/2 Red Chilli, Finely Chopped</li><li>1 Red Onion, Finely Sliced</li><li>2Cm Ginger, Peeled And Grated</li><li>1 Tsp Ground Cumin</li><li>1 Tsp Thai Curry Paste</li><li>Salt And Ground Black Pepper</li><li> 1/2 Lemon, Juice Only (Optional)</li><li>2 Ciabatta Loaves (4 Large Slices Of Sourdough)</li><li>Handful Coriander Leaves</li></ul>

<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>Combine cabbage, carrot and onion in a medium bowl. Cover with clingfilm and refrigerate for 15 minutes. </li><li>Meanwhile, heat 2 tbsp oil in a frying pan over medium heat and saute mustard seeds until sputtering. Add banana slices and cook for 2 3 minutes, or until lightly golden on both sides. Add turmeric, stir and cook for 1 minute, or until fragrant. Set aside. </li><li>Heat remaining oil in a large frying pan over medium heat. Add chilli, onion and ginger and cook for 3 4 minutes, or until onion softens and browns slightly. Add cumin, curry paste, salt and pepper and cook, stirring, for 30 seconds. </li><li>Add banana mixture and toss until well combined. Remove from heat. Stir in lemon juice. </li><li>Preheat the oven to 400 f. </li><li>Slice bread into eight equal size slices. Spread a generous amount of yoghurt mayonnaise on each slice and divide coleslaw evenly between them. Bake in the oven for 15 minutes, or until yoghurt is golden and bubbling. Garnish with chopped coriander. </li></ul>
