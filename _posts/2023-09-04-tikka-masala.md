<h2>Ingredients With Quantity </h2>
<ul><li>2 Medium Potatoes, Peeled And Roughly Chopped</li><li>100G/3 1/2Oz Puffed Rice (Also Known As Kurmura)</li><li>80G/2 3/4Oz Tomato, Finely Chopped (Approximately 1 Medium Tomato)</li><li>100G/3 1/2Oz Red Onion, Finely Chopped (Approximately 1 Small Onion)</li><li>Handful Roasted Peanuts</li><li>100G/3 1/2Oz Sev Or Bombay Mix</li><li>1 Tsp Chaat Masala</li><li>3 Tbsp Mint Chutney</li><li>3 Tbsp Tamarind Chutney</li><li>Pinch Salt</li><li>Handful Fresh Coriander Leaves</li><li>Handful Papdi, Roughly Crushed</li></ul>

<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>Take the onions and make fine rings and fry the onions till they turn golden in colour. </li><li>Crush the peanuts in your food processor or blender. </li><li>Drain the potatoes. </li><li>Preheat a pan and add the oil. </li><li>Add the potatoes, mix in to pan and sprinkle salt. </li><li>Cover with lid, cook till potatoes are cooked and start the frying. </li><li>Now add puffed rice kurmura and puffed rice to the pan, stir and make sure that no scum has formed. </li><li>Fry and stir to mix in all ingredients. </li><li>Add the tomato chutney, mint chutney, tamarind chutney, peanuts, salt. </li><li>Mix everything thoroughly. </li><li>Mix in the sev bombay mix. </li><li>Cook on medium heat for 1 minute and then remove from heat. </li><li>Now for the topping. </li><li>Garnish with coriander and papdi/pistachio slices. </li><li>Keep aside and let it cool and chill in the fridge. </li><li>Serve with indian flatbread or grilled bread with tomato chutneys and chutney. </li></ul>
