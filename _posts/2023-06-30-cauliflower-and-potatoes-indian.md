<h2>Ingredients With Quantity </h2>
<ul><li>3 Medium-Large Floury Potatoes (About 500G/1Lb 2Oz In Weight), Peeled And Chopped Into Bite-Sized Pieces</li><li>1 Tbsp Vegetable Oil</li><li>1 Large Brown Onion, Finely Chopped</li><li>2 Tsp Cumin Seeds</li><li>1 Star Anise</li><li>2 Tsp Sea Salt, Plus Extra If Desired</li><li>2 Large Garlic Cloves, Crushed</li><li>4Cm/1 1/2In Piece Fresh Root Ginger, Peeled And Finely Grated</li><li> 1/2 Chilli, Finely Chopped, Plus Extra If Desired</li><li>1 1/2 Tsp Garam Masala</li><li>1 Tsp Turmeric</li><li>Few Sprigs Fresh Coriander, Leaves And Stalks Separated And Both Finely Chopped (About 1 Pinch Stalks, 2 Pinches Leaves, Or To Taste)</li><li>50G/1 3/4Oz Unsalted Butter</li><li>1 Small Head Of Cauliflower, Cut Into Small Florets</li></ul>

<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>Boil the potatoes in salted water for about 25 30 mins, till they are just cooked but still firm to the touch. Drain and cool slightly. </li><li>Remove the skin from the potatoes and slice thinly. </li><li>Heat oil in a pan, add onions and fry for a few minutes till soft. Add cumin seeds, star anise, 2/3 tsp salt, cloves and ginger and cook over a medium heat for 3 4 mins. </li><li>Add chilli, garam masala, turmeric, coriander leaves and potato, stir gently for 5 6 mins till the potato flakes are crisp and coated with the spice paste. </li><li>Stir in the butter. </li><li>Heat a small, heavy based frying pan over a high heat and brown the cauliflower florets in batches. </li><li>Arrange on a serving dish, sprinkle with more salt and serve. </li></ul>
