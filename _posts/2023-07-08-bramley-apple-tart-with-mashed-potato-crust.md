<h2>Ingredients With Quantity </h2>
<ul><li>1.5 Litres/2 3/4 Pints Full-Fat Milk</li><li>450G/1Lb Caster Sugar</li><li>750Ml/1 1/4 Pints Double Cream</li><li>75G/2 3/4Oz Cornflour</li><li>Pinch Fine Salt</li><li>200G/7Oz Strong White Flour, Plus Extra For Dusting</li><li> 1/2 Tsp Fine Salt</li><li>185G/6 1/2Oz Unsalted Butter, Frozen</li><li>4 Tbsp Ice Cold Water</li><li>2 Tsp White Wine Vinegar</li><li>200G/7Oz Caster Sugar</li><li>30G/1Oz Unsalted Butter</li><li>2-3 Rosemary Sprigs</li><li>1-2 Bay Leaves</li><li>6 Bramley Or Cox Apples, Peeled, Cored And Halved</li></ul>

<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>Cook the potatoes in a large saucepan of boiling salted water for 10 minutes. Drain well and return to the pan with the milk and sugar. Bring to the boil and cook over a medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the liquid thickens. </li><li>For the mashed potato mixture, heat the cream, cornflour and salt to a boil in a saucepan over a medium heat, stirring, until the mixture boils and thickens. </li><li>Place the potato mixture into a bowl and mash well. Refrigerate overnight. </li><li>For the pastry, sift the flour and salt together into a large bowl. Rub in the butter until the mixture resembles breadcrumbs. Drizzle with ice water and mix to a soft dough. Knead the dough until smooth. Divide the pastry into 8 portions and shape each portion into a ball. </li><li>Roll out a ball of dough on a floured surface to 1/16th of an inch thick. Transfer the pastry to a pie tin. Press the pastry into the base and sides of the tin and trim the edges. Chill for 15 minutes. </li><li>Preheat the oven to 190c. </li><li>Combine the vinegar, sugar, butter, thyme and bay leaves in a small saucepan and bring to the boil, stirring until the sugar has dissolved. Pour over the apples and mix well. Place the tin on a baking tray and bake for 1 hour, until the pastry is golden. Cool. </li><li>Serve the apple tart with the mashed potato. </li></ul>
