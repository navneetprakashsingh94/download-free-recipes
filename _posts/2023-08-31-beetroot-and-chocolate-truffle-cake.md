<h2>Ingredients With Quantity </h2>
<ul><li>3 Large Free-Range Eggs</li><li>50G/1 3/4Oz Cocoa Powder</li><li>150G/5 1/2Oz Self-Raising Flour</li><li>2 Tsp Baking Powder</li><li>175G/6Oz Light Muscovado Sugar</li><li>300Ml/ 1/2 Pint Sunflower Oil, Plus Extra For Greasing</li><li>225G/8Oz Raw Beetroot, Peeled And Coarsely Grated (About 2 Medium Beetroots)</li><li>200G/7Oz Milk Or Dark Chocolate (About 35-40% Cocoa Solids), Broken Into Pieces</li><li>200Ml/7Fl Oz Pouring Double Cream</li><li>50G/1 3/4Oz White Chocolate, Chopped</li></ul>

<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>Grease and line a 10 inch square baking tin with baking paper. </li><li>Beat the eggs to light and fluffy and, if using a mixer, beat in the cocoa powder. </li><li>Fold through the flour, baking powder and sugar, a little at a time. </li><li>Stir in the oil, then the grated beetroot. </li><li>Transfer to prepared tin and bake for 1 hour at 170c. </li><li>Remove from oven, leave to cool slightly and refrigerate until well chilled. </li><li>Cut the cake into triangles and place in the cake tin. </li><li>Melt the chocolate atop a double boiler. </li><li>Cool slightly and stir until smooth. </li><li>Place the cream in a saucepan and bring to the boil. </li><li>Remove from heat and add the chocolate. </li><li>Stir to combine. </li><li>Pour over the cake and leave to set. </li><li>Turn onto a serving plate. </li><li>Decorate with the melted chocolate and serve. </li></ul>
