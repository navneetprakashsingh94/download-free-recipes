<h2>Ingredients With Quantity </h2>
<ul><li>12-15 Fresh Or Dried Curry Leaves</li><li>500G/1Lb 2Oz Onions, Roughly Chopped</li><li>2 Tbsp Rapeseed Oil</li><li>200G/7Oz Thick Natural Yoghurt</li><li>3 Tbsp Tomato Puree</li><li>2 X 5Cm/2In Fresh Root Ginger, Peeled And Finely Chopped</li><li>4-6 Garlic Cloves, Finely Chopped</li><li>1 Heaped Tsp Ground Cumin</li><li>2 Heaped Tsp Ground Coriander</li><li>1 Heaped Tbsp Chilli Powder</li><li>1 Tsp Ground Turmeric</li><li>500G/1Lb 2Oz Beef Rump Steak, Cut Into 2.5Cm/1In Cubes, Rinsed</li><li>4-5 Butternut Berries Or Pitted Plums</li><li>400Ml/16Fl Oz Tin Coconut Milk</li><li>1 Tsp Garam Masala (Optional)</li><li>2-3 Green Finger Chillies, Split Into Four Lengthways,</li><li>Salt And Freshly Ground Black Pepper</li><li>1 Tsp Rapeseed Oil Or Ghee</li><li>3Cm/1In Cinnamon Stick</li><li>2-3 Cardamom Pods, Lightly Crushed</li><li>2-3 Cloves</li><li>1 Tsp Cumin Seeds</li><li>1 Small Onion, Thinly Sliced</li><li>500G/1Lb 2Oz Basmati Or Long-Grain Rice</li><li>1 Litre/1 3/4 Pints Boiling Water</li></ul>

<h2>Directions</h2>
<ul><li>In a food processor, mix the curry leaves, onions, yoghurt, tomato puree, ginger, cloves, cumin, coriander, chilli powder and turmeric. Season the beef with salt and pepper and coat well with the mix. </li><li>Transfer the paste to a large plastic bag. Add the beef and rub all over with the paste. Set aside for 2 hours to marinate. </li><li>Preheat the oven to 325 f. Place the beef and marinade in a roasting tin and bake for 2 hours. </li><li>Add the butternut berries, stock, coconut milk and garam masala. Bring to the boil, reduce the heat and simmer gently for 30 minutes. </li><li>Add the beef, chilies and chopped onion and simmer for another 30 minutes. Season to taste and serve over basmati rice. </li><li>To make the basmati rice, heat the oil in a wok or large saucepan on medium heat. Add the cinnamon, cardamom and cloves and cumin seeds. Stir until the cumin seeds are sizzling and they begin to crackle. Add the onion and stir fry for 5 minutes, until the onion has softened and turned translucent. </li><li>Stir in the rice and fry for a further 1 2 minutes, until the rice turns</li></ul>
